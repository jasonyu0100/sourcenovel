# Ignition

## Genre
Supernatural Romance — Emotion-linked superpowers in a high school hierarchy. Explicit romantic and sexual tension with a love triangle at the core. Heat escalates fast — characters touch, want, and act on desire. Episodic format optimized for short-form engagement.

## Protagonist

### Rei Ashford
The untouchable Class-S prodigy. Rei's power is perfect control — she maintains absolute emotional suppression, which manifests as ice-cold precision. Her Flame Class ranking is the highest in academy history because she feels nothing. Or so everyone believes.

Beneath the composure is a furnace she's been damping since childhood. Her parents were both high-ranked; they taught her that feeling was failure. The earring she wears — crystalline, inherited from her mother — frosts over when she suppresses hard. It's the only tell she can't eliminate.

When Kade Morrow walks past her in the hall, her power flickers for the first time in years. That terrifies her more than anything.

### Kade Morrow
The Class-D burnout who feels everything. Kade's power is overwhelming because he can't stop feeling — heat radiates from him when emotional, the air shimmers, nearby surfaces warm to the touch. The academy classified him as unstable. He's been told his whole life that he's "too much."

The burn scars on his hands aren't from combat training. They're from the years he tried to feel less. He stopped trying.

Now he doesn't care about rankings, social hierarchy, or the rules that say someone like him can't look at someone like Rei. He looks anyway. And she looks back.

## Premise

At Apex Academy, your Flame Class determines everything — social standing, dorm assignment, future prospects, who you're allowed to touch. The ranking system measures emotional control: the more you suppress, the higher you rise. Class-S students are ice. Class-D students burn.

But the system has a fatal flaw. Desire is the most volatile emotion — and the one they're least equipped to handle.

Cross-class relationships are forbidden because emotional entanglement destabilizes power. When the pressure builds between incompatible ranks, both parties lose control. S-Class students fall. D-Class students spike dangerously. The hierarchy cracks.

Professor Vance knows this because he broke the rule fifteen years ago. His wife left after the scandal. He still wears the ring. He teaches Control Theory now, watching students make the same mistakes.

When Rei starts seeking Kade out — against every rule, every instinct, every lesson she's ever learned — the question isn't whether she'll lose control. It's what happens when she does.

## Setting

### Apex Academy
Elite institution for training emotion-powered individuals. Located in a climate-controlled zone to manage power fluctuations. The architecture reflects hierarchy:

- **S-Class Tower** — Pristine glass and steel, climate-controlled to 62°F, minimal decoration, maximum control
- **A/B-Class Hall** — Modern, comfortable, aspirational
- **C-Class Dorms** — Functional, crowded, overlooked
- **D-Class Block** — Utilitarian concrete, heat-scarred walls, deliberately uncomfortable

Key locations:
- **The Ranking Board** — Public display updated weekly. Everyone's status visible.
- **Training Grounds** — Where students learn to suppress. Temperature monitors everywhere.
- **The Cooling Room** — Mandatory for S-Class. Sub-zero meditation space.
- **The Basement** — Where D-Class students go when they "spike." No one talks about it.

### Power System: Flame Class
Emotion-linked abilities measured by control, not raw power. The ranking inverts traditional power fantasy:

- **S-Class**: Perfect suppression. Cold. Valued by society.
- **A-Class**: Strong control. Occasional flickers. Respectable.
- **B-Class**: Average. Functional. Invisible.
- **C-Class**: Emotional leakage. Empaths, sensitives. Useful but pitied.
- **D-Class**: Unstable. "Burnouts." Dangerous. Marginalized.

The secret: D-Class students often have the most raw power. They're just punished for feeling it.

## Central Themes

**Desire as Power** — The thing they're taught to suppress is also the source of their greatest strength. Control isn't power. Authenticity is.

**Hierarchy and Forbidden Attraction** — Class differences create instant stakes. Every charged moment is also political. Wanting someone you shouldn't is both romance and rebellion.

**The Cost of Suppression** — Rei has everything society values and feels nothing. Kade has nothing society values and feels everything. Who's actually free?

**Coming of Age Through Acceptance** — Growing up means accepting what you feel, not learning to hide it better.

## Core Engagement Hook

**Heat that delivers.**

Every scene asks: how far will they go? The visual language — ice melting, heat shimmering, composure shattering — is explicitly charged. Power effects are foreplay. Temperature is touch. When control breaks, the payoff is physical, immediate, and satisfying.

The love triangle creates a second axis of tension: Senna has been pursuing Rei for two years with possessive heat, and Kade undoes Rei's control in thirty seconds. Senna's claim vs. Kade's liberation. Ownership vs. desire. The hierarchy makes every encounter forbidden — and that makes every touch hotter.

## Series Identity

Ignition is smut romance optimized for short-form visual storytelling. Each episode delivers heat — physical contact, escalating intimacy, power effects as sex. The tension isn't whether they'll touch; it's what happens when they do.

The love triangle is the engine: Senna wants to own Rei, Kade wants to free her, and Rei is discovering what she actually wants for the first time. Every episode escalates one of these relationships while complicating the others.

The aesthetic is modern anime with explicit romantic themes — Chainsaw Man's simple designs and bold expressions, Jujutsu Kaisen's dynamic visual language, the heat of a romance that knows exactly what it wants and isn't afraid to show it.

The format is episodic: standalone heat with serialized relationship progression. Entry points everywhere. Every episode works as a TikTok hook. Every arc delivers payoff — not teases, payoff.

Audience: Adults who want supernatural romance with real heat, explicit tension that escalates to contact, a compelling love triangle, and characters hot enough to obsess over.
