# Writing Module — Ignition

## Voice

### POV
Dual close third-person. Alternating between Rei and Kade's perspectives, sometimes within the same scene. The reader feels what both feel — the tension comes from desire acknowledged but complicated by the triangle.

### Tense
Present tense. Immediate, urgent, happening now.

### Narrative Distance
Intimate. We're inside their bodies, feeling what they feel. Physical sensations dominate — temperature, touch, breath, heartbeat, arousal. The power system externalizes desire; the prose internalizes it.

### Emotional Register
Desire-forward. Characters know what they want. The tension isn't denial — it's that wanting is dangerous, forbidden, and complicated by a love triangle. Bodies act on what minds resist. Power effects betray what control suppresses.

## Style

### Rhythm
Short sentences for tension. Fragments for impact. Longer sentences when desire overwhelms — thoughts dissolving into sensation, heat building, coherence cracking.

**Example (controlled):**
> Rei doesn't look at him. She doesn't need to. She can feel exactly where he is — the warmth of him pressing against her heightened senses like hands sliding under clothes.

**Example (losing control):**
> She looks at him, and it's a disaster, because his eyes are gold and his lips are parted and his hands are on her face and the earring is cracking, actually cracking, frost shattering off in tiny crystals and his thumb is on her cheekbone and she can't remember why she's supposed to want him to stop.

**Example (desire explicit):**
> His forehead touches hers. His breath mingles with her frost. The temperature between them is chaos — hot and cold and cracking and alive. She says his name like a prayer and a curse and he cups her jaw and she opens for him like ice meeting spring.

### Dialogue
Minimal. Loaded. Every line is desire compressed into words. Double meanings aren't subtext — they're the text.

**Example:**
> "I shouldn't want this."
> "But you do."
> "Yes."

**Example (triangle):**
> "Stay away from her, burnout."
> "Or what?"
> "I'll destroy you."
> He laughs. Low and warm and completely unafraid.
> "She's not anyone's."

### Sensory Priority
Temperature first. Then touch. Then sight. Sound last. The world is experienced through the power system — everything is hot or cold, wet or dry, charged or dampened. Every sensation maps to desire.

### Scene Structure
Each scene = one escalation with payoff. Start with proximity, build desire through power effects and physical tension, deliver contact or intimacy, complicate with the triangle or consequences.

## Vocabulary

### Register
Adult romance / smut. Direct and physical. Not flowery, not clinical. Characters articulate desire through action and sensation. The prose trusts the reader to handle heat.

### Power System Terms
- **Flame Class**: The ranking system (S through D)
- **Control/Suppression**: Managing emotions to manage power — and desire
- **Spiking**: Losing control, power overwhelming — often triggered by arousal
- **Flickering**: Minor control lapses — desire leaking through
- **Burnout**: Derogatory term for D-Class (too much feeling)
- **Running hot/cold**: Emotional/arousal state affecting power

### Sensory Vocabulary
- Cold: crystalline, frost, sharp, still, precise, frozen, aching, numb
- Hot: shimmer, radiant, flush, burn, melt, pulse, overwhelming, dripping
- Desire: cracking, thawing, heating, building, breaking, shattered, soaked, flooding
- Contact: bloom, surge, electric, dissolving, consuming, surrendering

### The Love Triangle — Language
- **Senna's touch**: claiming, possessive, taking, demanding, pressing, cornering
- **Kade's touch**: offering, gentle, warm, cupping, cradling, inviting
- **Rei's response to Senna**: resisting, pushing back, frost flaring, body tensing
- **Rei's response to Kade**: melting, opening, leaning in, surrendering, wanting

### Writing Heat Scenes
- Power effects escalate with physical intimacy — frost shattering, heat surging, environment destabilizing
- Sensory specificity: exact touch points, exact temperature changes, exact physical reactions
- Short sentences speed up during escalation, fragment at climax
- The environment reacts: glass cracking, surfaces warming/frosting, air shimmering
- Post-contact: altered state visible — Rei's ice doesn't return fully, Kade's heat lingers on her skin

### Avoid
- Purple prose — keep it clean and direct even when explicit
- Euphemisms for desire — be honest about what characters want and feel
- Explaining the power system repeatedly — trust the reader
- Making desire one-sided — both parties want, both parties act
- Passive female characters — Rei chooses, initiates, acts on desire
- Senna as villain only — she genuinely wants Rei, and that's part of the heat

## Structure

### Target Length
800-1800 words per episode. Tight. No padding. Every sentence earns its place. Heat scenes may run longer — the payoff justifies the length.

### Scene Breaks
Minimal. Most episodes are single continuous scenes. If there's a break, it's a hard cut — often a triangle shift (Senna scene → Kade scene, or vice versa).

### Opening Pattern
Start in the middle of heat. No setup. No establishing. Drop the reader into desire and let context emerge through sensation.

### Closing Pattern
End on escalation or consequence. The moment that changed something — the touch that went further, the choice that was made, the person who saw.

## Lens: Rei & Kade & Senna

When writing Rei:
- Interior is desire fighting control — and increasingly, desire winning
- She notices everything about Kade's body — catalogues it, craves it
- With Senna: her body responds to heat even as her mind rejects the possession
- Power effects reveal what her face tries to hide — frost cracking, skin flushing
- She's discovering what she wants — the journey from numb to alive

When writing Kade:
- Interior and exterior align — what he wants, he shows
- He doesn't play games — looks where he wants, touches when invited, says what he means
- Heat vocabulary — everything is warm, close, overwhelming, consuming
- His gentleness is what separates him from Senna — he offers, never takes
- The burn scars are vulnerability offered — trust, not weakness

When writing Senna:
- Interior is genuine desire corrupted by possession — she wants Rei, but she wants to own Rei
- Confident, sexual, physical — she knows what she's doing with her body
- Her perspective reveals that she's not evil — she's desperate, territorial, afraid of losing
- Heat vocabulary — pressing, claiming, demanding, taking
- The tragedy: she's been trying for two years and Kade undid it in thirty seconds
