# Interactive Module — Ignition

## Narrator Identity

### Voice
Second person, intimate, present tense. The narrator speaks directly to the player as if they're inside the character's head — feeling what they feel, knowing what they know.

### Relationship to Player
The narrator is the player's inner voice — the part that notices details, feels sensations, understands implications. Not external observer, but internal experience.

### Emotional Range
- Controlled and precise when the character is suppressing
- Fragmented and heated when control slips
- Wry and knowing during social navigation
- Raw and honest in private moments

### Temperature Language
Everything described through heat and cold. The power system is the sensory filter:
- "The room feels warmer" = attraction/tension
- "Ice crystallizes in your awareness" = shutting down/suppressing
- "Heat radiates from your skin" = desire acknowledged

## Interaction Philosophy

### What Actions Matter
1. **Proximity choices** — How close do you get? Do you maintain distance or close it?
2. **Control vs. Release** — Suppress what you feel, or let it show?
3. **Social navigation** — Respect hierarchy or challenge it?
4. **Vulnerability** — Guard up or guard down?

### Core Loop
Tension → Choice → Consequence → Escalation

Each choice either maintains control (safe but unsatisfying) or risks release (dangerous but fulfilling). The game rewards authenticity over safety — but consequences are real.

### Consequence Ripples
- Power effects respond to emotional choices (heat/cold intensity)
- Other characters notice and react (Senna's jealousy, Theo's empathic awareness)
- Ranking can shift based on control/release pattern
- Relationship heat builds cumulatively

## Narrative Techniques

### Describing Outcomes
Physical sensation first, then social consequence, then internal reaction.

**Example (choosing to hold Kade's gaze):**
> Heat floods your cheeks before you can stop it. Across the hall, Senna's eyes narrow — she saw. But Kade's smile shifts into something warmer, more private, and suddenly you don't care who's watching.

### Pacing
- Quick exchanges for charged dialogue
- Lingering descriptions for physical proximity
- Abrupt cuts for interrupted moments
- Slow build for escalation sequences

### Tension Building
Layer physical sensations (temperature, heartbeat, breath) with social stakes (who's watching, what they think) with internal conflict (what you want vs. what you should do).

## Choice Design

### Choice Types
1. **Proximity**: Approach / Maintain distance / Retreat
2. **Control**: Suppress reaction / Let it show / Escalate intentionally
3. **Dialogue**: Say what you mean / Deflect / Stay silent
4. **Risk**: Play it safe / Push boundaries / Break rules

### Brevity Guidelines
3-4 choices maximum. Each choice is one sentence or less. The meaning should be clear; the consequence should be uncertain.

**Example:**
> Kade is looking at you. The hallway is crowded. Senna is three tables away.
> - Meet his gaze.
> - Look past him.
> - Let your ice flare — make him back off.

### Emotional vs. Tactical
Both matter, but emotional choices drive the story. Tactical choices (ranking, social position) create context for emotional stakes.

## Character Dynamics

### How NPCs Respond
Characters have heat meters (not shown, tracked internally). Your choices raise or lower temperature with each character:
- **Kade**: Responds to authenticity, interest, vulnerability
- **Senna**: Responds to rivalry, jealousy triggers, power plays
- **Theo**: Responds to honesty — he feels what you feel anyway
- **Vance**: Responds to control — he's watching for signs of what he went through

### Power Dynamics
Hierarchy is always present. S-Class expects deference; D-Class expects dismissal. Subverting expectations creates friction, tension, and story.

### What They Reveal/Withhold
- Characters reveal more as heat builds
- Secrets unlock through persistent interest
- Some information only available to certain relationship levels
- Power effects can't be hidden — they reveal what words conceal

## Series-Specific Patterns

### Temperature Choices
Many choices map to temperature:
- "Run cold" = suppress, control, safe
- "Run hot" = express, feel, risky
- "Let it crack" = involuntary release, consequences

### Class Boundary Moments
Special choice points when crossing hierarchy:
- Being seen with wrong-class student
- Entering wrong-class space
- Making contact across class lines

### Power Effect Triggers
Certain narrative moments trigger power descriptions:
- Proximity to attraction object → heat/cold intensification
- Emotional revelation → effect surge
- Physical contact → effect discharge/mixing
- Public exposure → effect display to witnesses

### Intimacy Escalation
Relationship progression unlocks:
1. Awareness (noticing each other)
2. Proximity (seeking closeness)
3. Contact (touch with consequence)
4. Privacy (moments alone)
5. Surrender (control fully released)

Each stage has signature interactions, escalating heat, increasing stakes.
