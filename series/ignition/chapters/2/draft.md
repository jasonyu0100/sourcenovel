# The Fallout

Rei's control is failing.

Hours after the storage room, her cold keeps flickering — frost forming on her fingertips, then melting. Forming. Melting. The temperature regulation she's spent years perfecting, shot in thirty seconds of contact with a D-Class burnout whose hands felt like sunrise.

She needs the infirmary. Needs someone to stabilize what Kade's touch destabilized.

The cracked earring hangs visible against her silver hair. Evidence. She should have taken it off, hidden the damage, but she can't stop touching it. Can't stop remembering the way it shattered when he cupped her face and she *let* him.

*I shouldn't want this.*

*But you do.*

*Yes.*

The infirmary is quiet this late. Clinical white walls. Antiseptic cold that should feel familiar but doesn't — nothing feels cold enough anymore. Not since him.

Mira Chen takes her intake.

Soft curves hidden beneath an oversized cardigan. Warm brown eyes behind glasses. Dark hair in a messy bun with strands framing her face. B-Class healer, Theo's older sister — Rei knows her by reputation, by the way patients request her specifically. Her power is Transfer: she absorbs emotional overflow to accelerate healing.

"Your control signature is destabilized." Mira's voice is gentle. Professional. "I'll need physical contact to assess the damage."

Rei nods. Extends her hand.

The moment Mira's fingers touch her wrist, everything changes.

Mira gasps. Her eyes widen behind fogging glasses, brown irises darkening as Transfer activates — flooding her with fragments of what happened in the storage room. Rei feels it happening. Feels Mira feeling *her*.

Amber heat. Hands cupping her face. The earring cracking, ice crystals scattering. *"There you are."* The terror and the thaw and the wanting, all of it rushing into the healer like a wave she can't stop.

Mira's cheeks flush pink. Her lips part. She's absorbing a secret that could destroy the S-1 ranked student at Apex Academy — forbidden desire for a D-Class, cross-class contact that breaks every rule — and she can't stop feeling it.

*His thumb on her cheekbone. His forehead against hers. The temperature between them chaos and cracking and alive.*

Mira feels what Rei felt. Kade's warmth spreading through her borrowed sensations. The way Rei's ice shattered not from force but from *relief*. The hunger beneath the frost, finally freed.

Their eyes meet.

Rei sees the recognition in Mira's darkened gaze. The healer knows. Knows everything — the storage room, the touch, the wanting. It's written in the flush spreading from her chest to her cheeks, in the way her fingers tremble against Rei's wrist.

The silence between them is loaded. Dangerous.

Mira's lips part like she might speak. She doesn't. She finishes the healing with hands that shake slightly, and when she pulls away, Rei understands: the healer carries pieces of her desire now. Her secret lives in someone else's body.

Neither of them acknowledges what passed between them.

Nina Volkov appears in the doorway.

Platinum white hair cut in a sharp asymmetrical bob. Pale blue eyes still hazy, unfocused. Nurse uniform that's slightly too small — white dress straining at the chest, hem riding high. She looks flushed. Breathless. Like she just finished something.

"Shift handoff." Her voice is lazy. Satisfied. "How's our S-Class patient?"

She was on intake earlier, Rei remembers. When she first arrived with her control shattered, Nina sedated her with Drain — siphoned the emotional overflow to nothing, left her calm and drowsy. The rush of that dissipation must have hit the nurse like a drug.

The suppressed desire Nina drained from her. Years of wanting frozen down, suddenly thawed and leaking. Nina got high on *that*.

Now she's watching Mira with knowing amusement. Watching the flush on the healer's cheeks. The fog on her glasses.

"Something interesting happening here," Nina murmurs. Not a question.

She intends to pay attention.

---

Kade finds the D-Class hallway empty.

Or it should be. He's barely made it three steps before someone blocks his path.

Pretty. Dangerous. Androgynous features that blur beautiful and handsome. Dark auburn hair falling across one eye, deliberately styled. Green eyes that match Senna's — *family*, Kade realizes. Lean build with a dancer's grace. Silver rings on every finger, catching the amber light.

"Kade Morrow." The voice is cold. Precise. "My cousin sends a message."

Senna's enforcer. Her weapon.

"Stay away from Rei Ashford." Each word is deliberate. "Or face destruction. Your ranking. Your future. Everything you might have had — gone. She will burn you to nothing for touching what's hers."

The devotion bleeds through every syllable. Not just following orders. *Believing* them.

Kade laughs.

The same laugh he gave Senna in the storage room, when she threatened to destroy him for daring to touch her trophy. Low and warm and completely unafraid.

"She's not anyone's."

Jace's patience snaps. His hand shoots out, grabbing Kade's arm — bare skin to bare skin, no rings between them.

Echo activates.

The mirror opens and they both fall in.

Jace feels Kade's Radiance flood into him. The heat. The certainty. The all-in devotion to a silver-haired ice queen who shattered in his hands and whispered *yes*. It pours through the connection, overwhelming, undeniable.

Kade feels Jace's devotion echoed back. Desperate. Complicated. The way he follows Senna's orders not just from family loyalty but from something else — something that blurs cousin and *want*. The ache of serving someone who will never see him the way he needs.

Both sensations bounce between them. Amplifying. Escalating with each pass until neither can tell whose feeling is whose. Jace's devotion. Kade's heat. Jace's desperate wanting. Kade's fearless certainty. All of it tangled, mirrored, feeding back.

Jace releases him like he's been burned.

Steps back. Breathing hard. The silver rings clink as his hands shake — grounding techniques failing, Echo still echoing through his nervous system.

"The warning is delivered." His voice cracks. "Stay away from her."

He turns. Walks away too fast.

He'll report to Senna that the message was sent. He won't mention the rest. The way Kade's desire felt like his own. The heat still lingering under his skin hours later.

Something changed in that hallway. Jace doesn't have words for what.

---

Kade needs space.

He finds the greenhouse wing. The only place D-Class can breathe without surveillance. Ventilation systems mask heat signatures. The isolation is by design — they keep someone here. Someone whose emissions require special containment.

He finds her among the plants.

Green hair falling in waves past her shoulders. Half-lidded golden eyes. Full pouty lips curved in lazy amusement. Voluptuous figure she doesn't bother hiding — crop top uniform showing midriff, skirt riding high. Vine-like tattoo curling up her thigh.

Ivy Thornwood. D-Class Pheromone. Isolated because she can't control what she exudes.

Her power reaches for him automatically. Chemical desire, manufactured wanting — the thing that makes everyone who visits leave craving more.

His heat incinerates it before it reaches him.

The pheromones burn away in his Radiance. Nothing but clear air between them. No manipulation. No chemical advantage.

Ivy's eyes widen. Golden, surprised, *interested*.

"You're immune." Her voice is husky. Amused. "That's new."

"Heat burns it off." He shrugs. "I run too hot."

"The ice queen freezes it. But you..." She tilts her head, studying him. "You burn it away. That's different."

She's never met anyone like him. Everyone else either succumbs or resists through cold suppression. Kade just *is* — too much heat for her chemistry to take hold.

For the first time, Ivy is just herself. No manufactured desire between them. No power games. Just a D-Class burnout meeting another burnout in the only place either of them can exist.

"You're the one who touched the S-1." Not a question. News travels.

"Yeah."

"Bold." Her lips curve. "Senna Vale wants you dead."

"Senna Vale can want whatever she wants. Rei chose me."

Something flickers in Ivy's expression. Recognition, maybe. One outcast seeing another. Both of them punished for what they can't contain — her leaking pheromones, him radiating heat, the system labeling them dangerous for being too much.

Neither makes a move. Not yet.

But Ivy's watching him with genuine interest now. Not desire manufactured by chemistry. Something real.

---

Professor Cross stands in the shadows of the administrative wing.

Her power is Perception — emotional signatures visible through walls, colors bleeding through architecture. She watches it all unfold.

Mira's colors shifting as she absorbs Rei's secret. Pink flooding into her signature, desire transferred. Nina's drugged haze in the doorway, pale satisfaction at the high she's riding.

Jace and Kade's confrontation. Auburn and amber tangling, Echo reflecting Radiance until neither signature is pure anymore. Jace walking away changed, carrying heat that isn't his.

The greenhouse. Kade's amber meeting Ivy's green. No interference. No power games. Just two burnouts finding each other in isolation.

Fifteen years ago, Cross watched Vance fall for a C-Class. Watched their colors merge exactly like Rei and Kade's did in the storage room — cold blue cracking, amber flooding in, something volatile and beautiful being born.

She didn't report it then.

The instability event hospitalized three people. Vance's power burned out. His career ended. His wife left. Cross told herself *never again*.

She's doing it again now.

Watching. Not reporting.

The secret binds her too.
