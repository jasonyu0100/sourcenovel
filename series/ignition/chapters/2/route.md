# Chapter 2 Route: The Fallout

Rei's control is failing. Hours after the storage room, her cold keeps flickering — frost forming then melting on her fingertips, temperature regulation shot. She needs the infirmary, needs someone to stabilize what Kade's touch destabilized. The cracked earring hangs visible, evidence of her shattered composure.

Mira Chen takes her intake. Soft curves, glasses, cardigan — invisible until you need her touch. She's Theo's sister, B-Class healer, works the day shift. Her power is Transfer: she absorbs emotional overflow to accelerate healing. She reaches for Rei's hand.

The moment Mira's fingers touch Rei's wrist, Transfer activates. She gasps — flooded with fragments of what happened in the storage room. Amber heat. Hands cupping her face. The earring cracking. "I shouldn't want this." The terror and the thaw and the wanting, all of it rushing into Mira like a wave.

Mira feels everything Rei felt. Kade's warmth spreading through her. The forbidden desire for a D-Class. The way Rei's ice shattered not from force but from relief. Mira's cheeks flush. Her glasses fog. She's absorbing a secret that could destroy the ice queen — and she can't stop feeling it.

Their eyes meet. Rei sees the recognition in Mira's darkened gaze — the healer knows. The silence between them is loaded. Mira's lips part like she might speak. She doesn't. She finishes the healing with hands that tremble slightly, carrying pieces of Rei's desire inside her now.

Nina Volkov appears in the doorway. Night shift handoff. Platinum hair, nurse uniform too small, pale blue eyes still hazy. She was on intake earlier — sedated Rei with her Drain when the S-Class first arrived. The suppressed desire she siphoned hit her like a drug. She's still riding the high, watching Mira with knowing amusement. Something delicious is happening. Nina intends to pay attention.

---

Jace Delacroix finds Kade in the D-Class hallways. Pretty and dangerous — androgynous features, auburn hair falling across one eye, silver rings on every finger. Senna's cousin. Senna's enforcer. He delivers the warning with cold precision: stay away from Rei, or face destruction. His devotion to Senna bleeds through every word.

Kade doesn't back down. Laughs, actually — the same laugh he gave Senna in the storage room. Jace's patience snaps. He grabs Kade's arm to emphasize the threat, bare skin to bare skin, and his Echo activates on contact.

The mirror opens. Jace feels Kade's Radiance flood into him — the heat, the certainty, the all-in devotion to Rei. Kade feels Jace's devotion to Senna echoed back — desperate, complicated, blurring family and something else. Both sensations bounce between them, amplifying with each pass. Neither can tell whose feeling is whose anymore.

Jace releases him like he's been burned. Steps back, breathing hard, rings clinking as his hands shake. He delivered the warning. He'll report that to Senna. He won't mention the rest — the way Kade's desire felt like his own, the heat still lingering under his skin. Something changed in that hallway. Jace doesn't have words for what.

---

Kade needs space. Retreats toward the greenhouse wing — the only place D-Class can breathe without surveillance. The ventilation systems mask heat signatures. The isolation is by design.

He finds someone already there. Green hair falling in waves, half-lidded golden eyes, vine tattoo curling up her thigh. Ivy Thornwood. D-Class Pheromone. Isolated because she can't control her emissions — everyone who visits leaves wanting more. She looks up as he enters, and her power reaches for him automatically.

His heat incinerates her pheromones before they reach him. The chemical desire burns away in his Radiance, leaving nothing but clear air between them. Ivy's eyes widen. She's never met anyone immune except Rei — and Rei's cold freezes her power. This is different. This is someone who burns it away.

For the first time, Ivy is just herself. No chemical advantage. No manipulation. Just a D-Class burnout meeting another burnout in the only place either of them can exist. They recognize something in each other — fellow outcasts, both punished for leaking what they can't contain. Neither makes a move. But Ivy's watching him with genuine interest now. Not desire manufactured by pheromones. Something real.

---

Professor Cross stands in the shadows of the administrative wing, watching emotional signatures through walls. She sees Mira's colors shift as she absorbs Rei's secret. Sees Nina's drugged haze in the doorway. Sees Jace and Kade's confrontation flare and tangle. Sees Kade and Ivy's signatures — his amber, her green — meeting in the greenhouse without interference.

Fifteen years ago, she watched Vance fall for a C-Class. Watched their colors merge exactly like Rei and Kade's did in the storage room. She didn't report it then. The instability event hospitalized three people. She told herself never again.

She's doing it again now. Watching. Not reporting. The secret binds her too.
