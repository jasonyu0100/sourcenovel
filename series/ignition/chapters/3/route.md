# Chapter 3 Route: Senna's Room

Senna's quarters, late night. She's pacing — uniform half-undone, skin damp, heat-haze pressing against the walls so thick the mirror fogs and stays fogged. The room is sweltering. She can't make it stop. Can't stop seeing it — his hands on Rei's face, the earring cracking, Rei's lips parting, the frost shattering off her like armor being stripped.

She stops at her vanity. The drawer. Inside: two years of Rei, curated. A scarf that still holds traces of frost — she brings it to her face and breathes in, cold silk against her mouth, hunting for the ghost of Rei's skin beneath the fabric. A photograph from the rankings ceremony. Senna's fingers leave heat marks on Rei's image. She traces Rei's collarbones in the photo the way she's imagined tracing them in person — slow, possessive, down.

The collar touch replays. Her thumb finding Rei's pulse that morning, pressing just hard enough to feel it quicken. "Let me warm you up." Two years of patience. Two years of reading Rei's resistance as foreplay — frost that would eventually melt if Senna just kept her hands warm enough, kept pressing close enough, kept breathing against her neck until the ice cracked. Now the reframing hits like poison: Rei wasn't resisting to be conquered. Rei was resisting because Senna's touch didn't make her want to stop resisting.

The storage room replays. Each loop worse. Kade's hands on Rei's face become Kade's hands sliding into Rei's hair, tilting her head back, Rei's throat exposed and wanting. The earring doesn't just crack — it shatters while Rei arches into him. Senna's version isn't what happened. It's what she's terrified happened. That Rei opened for him — opened the way Senna has spent two years trying to pry open with patient fingers and warm breath and Thrall pressed against every inch of available skin.

Thrall activates without permission. Heat floods her — not the controlled warmth she uses on others but raw, directionless want pooling low in her body, flushing up her chest, throat, face. The shimmer-haze thickens until the air is wet with it. Her body is reaching for Rei and Rei isn't here. She presses the scarf against her neck, then lower, imagining Rei's cold hands where the silk touches. The frost on the fabric melts against her skin — two years of Rei's cold dissolving under ten seconds of Senna's need. She imagines Rei shivering under her, ice cracking not from some D-Class burnout but from Senna's mouth on her throat, Senna's Thrall sinking through skin until Rei can't tell the difference between warmth and want.

She catches herself. Scarf clutched against her chest, breathing ragged, Thrall radiating to an empty room like a body performing for no one. This is what he reduced her to — wet and wanting in the dark, touching fabric instead of skin. She throws the scarf back in the drawer. Slams it shut. Won't give herself the release. That would mean admitting the distance is real — that she's alone with this, that Rei isn't on the other side of it, that the only person Senna is making desperate is herself.

Jace's report. She forces herself to think. Warning delivered, burnout didn't flinch, laughed the same way he laughed at her in the storage room. But Jace was different when he came back — flushed, hands shaking, rings clinking like wind chimes in a storm. She'd grabbed his wrist and asked what happened. He pulled away. Said nothing. She let him go because she needed the lie. Now, alone, the doubt eats: what did Kade's heat do to her cousin? Why did Jace come back looking like he'd been touched?

Strategy. She needs strategy, not sensation. Patience failed. Jace failed. Direct confrontation failed — Kade laughed and said words that are still cutting: "She wouldn't choose the girl who makes her feel like a trophy." Senna's nails dig into her palms. She needs to stop hearing his voice in her head. She can't. He sounds like he knows something about Rei that Senna doesn't. That's the part that makes her sick.

Dante. The thought lands like a blade unsheathed. Last year she had Dante aim Pulse near Rei — manufactured attraction, hoping jealousy would crack the ice. Failed. Rei felt nothing. But Rei feels things now. Kade broke the seal. Which means Dante's Pulse could work differently — not aimed at Rei, but orbiting Kade. Make everyone want him. Flood him with bodies and heat until Rei sees him drowning in manufactured desire and thinks: he's not special. He's not mine. He's everyone's.

She texts Dante. He arrives in minutes — because Dante always comes when Senna calls, the way pretty weapons come to the hand that wields them. He leans in the doorway, shirt open at the collar, Pulse simmering under his skin like an invitation. His eyes move over her: the undone uniform, the damp skin, the heat-haze filling the room, the drawer she didn't close all the way. He's seen Senna hungry before. Never like this. He can smell obsession the way sharks smell blood.

Senna pitches it standing too close to him — because proximity is how she negotiates, how she controls, Thrall leaking between them like foreplay. A D-Class burnout who touched what's hers. Needs to be made ordinary. Dante's Pulse aimed at the people around Kade — make him wanted by everyone so what Rei felt becomes common, cheap, nothing. Dante listens with his mouth curved and his eyes half-lidded and his Pulse brushing back against her Thrall in a way that makes the air between them shimmer. He doesn't ask why. He asks what's in it for him. She answers with a look that could be a promise or a threat and Dante reads both fluently.

But Dante is a professional reader of want. And what he sees in Senna right now — the heat-haze, the open drawer, the flush that won't recede, the way she's standing too close because she needs to be close to someone and Rei isn't available — isn't strategy. It's a woman being eaten alive by her own desire. He almost tells her. Almost says she's past the point where plans work. Instead he lets his Pulse brush her one more time — a professional courtesy, one manipulator to another — and pushes off the doorframe. "Tomorrow, then." The door closes. Senna stands in the heat he left behind and it's wrong, it's the wrong heat, it's not cold enough, not Rei enough, not anything.

Last thing before sleeping: the drawer again. The photograph. Rei's face, untouchable, perfectly composed, the jawline Senna has memorized with her eyes and never been allowed to memorize with her mouth. She traces it — the same path Kade's fingers took. Thrall blooms one final time, involuntary, unstoppable, her body still reaching for someone who isn't reaching back. "She's mine." The words come out broken. Not a claim anymore. A prayer to a god who's already chosen someone else.
