# Overview
Hours after the storage room, Senna is alone in her quarters with heat-haze she can't control and memories she can't stop replaying. The chapter follows her psychological descent from confident pursuer to desperate schemer — obsessive spiral, involuntary arousal, edged denial, and the decision to weaponize Dante against Kade.
> Senna's POV reveals the triangle's other edge. She's not a villain — she's a woman who spent two years learning someone's body language and was outpaced in thirty seconds by a boy who looked at the same ice and saw something Senna never found.

## The Fogged Mirror
Senna's quarters, sweltering. Heat-haze pressing against walls, fogging the mirror, uniform unbuttoned and sticking to damp skin. She can't regulate her own power. Can't stop seeing his hands on Rei's face, the earring cracking, Rei leaning into him.
> Senna's loss of thermal control mirrors her loss of emotional control. The A-2 ranked student who weaponizes warmth can't manage her own heat.
"She's making it sweltering. She can't stop."

## The Drawer
Senna opens the vanity drawer — a private altar of two years' obsession. Silk scarf with traces of frost, a bitten pen cap, a rankings ceremony photograph where Senna is barely visible at the frame's edge.
> The collected items make the obsession physical and visible. Each artifact is evidence of surveillance disguised as devotion — watching, memorizing, curating someone who never invited it.

### The Scarf
She breathes in the scarf, hunting for Rei's skin beneath the cold silk. Her fingers trace Rei's collarbones in the photograph — slow, possessive, down — leaving heat marks on the glossy surface.
> The scarf becomes a surrogate for the intimacy she's never been granted. Heat marks on the photograph literalize how Senna's desire damages what it touches.
"That clean frost-and-nothing scent that Senna has been chasing for two years."

## The Reframing
The collar touch replays — "Let me warm you up." Two years of reading Rei's resistance as foreplay, frost that would eventually melt with enough patience and proximity. Now the poisonous realization: Rei wasn't resisting to be conquered. Rei was resisting because Senna's touch didn't make her want to stop.
> The reframing is the chapter's psychological hinge. Senna's entire two-year narrative collapses — resistance wasn't the game, it was the answer. She just couldn't read it because reading it meant losing.
"But his did."

## The Distorted Loop
The storage room replays, each loop worse. Kade's hands become more intimate in Senna's memory — sliding into Rei's hair, tilting her head back, Rei arching into him. The earring doesn't just crack; it shatters while Rei opens for him.
> Senna's memory distorts toward her worst fear: that Rei didn't just break, she wanted to break. The fantasy she's terrorizing herself with is also the fantasy she's aroused by — Rei opening, surrendering, wanting. Just not for Senna.
"He did it in thirty seconds with his bare hands on her face and a voice that said *there you are* like he'd found something Senna didn't know existed."

## Thrall Unleashed
Thrall activates without permission — raw, directionless want flooding her body. Not the controlled warmth she uses on others. This is her power betraying how far gone she is.
> Involuntary Thrall is the chapter's key moment. Senna's power is designed to make others want; now it's manifesting her own want with no target, no control, no outlet. The seducer seduced by her own need.

### The Fantasy Dissolves
She presses Rei's scarf against her throat, then lower. Imagines Rei's cold hands where the silk touches. The frost melts against her skin. The line between memory and fantasy dissolves — she imagines Rei underneath her, ice cracking from Senna's mouth on her throat, Thrall sinking through skin.
> The fantasy reveals what Senna actually wants: not just Rei's body but Rei's surrender. She wants to be the one who cracks the ice. The tragedy is that she's imagining exactly what Kade already achieved.
"Two years of Rei's cold dissolving under ten seconds of Senna's need."

### The Denial
Senna catches herself — scarf clutched to bare chest, breathing wrecked, Thrall radiating to an empty room. She throws the scarf back, slams the drawer. Refuses release because release means admitting the distance is real.
> Edged denial as psychological defense. If she doesn't finish, she doesn't have to admit she's alone with this. The wanting stays theoretical. But the shaking hands buttoning her shirt say otherwise.
"Wanting in the dark. Touching fabric instead of skin."

## Jace's Cracks
She replays Jace's report — warning delivered, burnout laughed. But Jace came back different. Flushed. Trembling. He pulled away when she grabbed his wrist — pulled away from Senna, who he's never pulled away from.
> Senna let him go because she needed the lie. Now the doubt surfaces: what did Kade's heat do to her cousin? Jace's incomplete report is a crack in Senna's information network — she's scheming with bad intelligence.
"Why did Jace come back looking like he'd been touched by something he wanted?"

## The Trophy Wound
Strategy audit: patience failed, Jace failed, direct confrontation failed. Kade's words loop — "She wouldn't choose the girl who makes her feel like a trophy." The wound isn't that he touched Rei. It's that he saw her.
> Kade's parting shot from Chapter 1 is still cutting because it names what Senna won't admit: she's been staring at Rei's ice for two years and never found the wanting girl underneath. Someone else looked once and saw everything.
"He sounds like he knows something about Rei that Senna doesn't, and that's the part that makes her sick."

## Dante's Arrival
Senna conceives the plan — Dante's Pulse aimed not at Rei but around Kade. Make everyone want him. Make what Rei felt look cheap, common, nothing. She texts Dante. He arrives in minutes.
> The strategic pivot from seduction to sabotage. Senna can't melt through to Rei directly, so she'll devalue the competition. The plan is clever. It's also the first sign she's given up on being chosen.

### The Doorway
Dante leans in the doorway — shirt open, Pulse simmering like cologne. His eyes catalogue everything: the rebuttoned shirt she missed a button on, the damp skin, the heat-haze, the drawer she didn't close. He can smell obsession the way sharks smell blood.
> Dante is introduced as a professional reader of want — the perfect mirror for Senna's state. He's seen her hungry before. Never like this.
"Late night."

### Two Manipulators
Senna pitches standing too close, Thrall leaking between them like foreplay. Dante's Pulse brushes back against her Thrall — two powers designed to make people want, pressing against each other in a room that already smells like desire. He doesn't ask why. He asks what's in it for him.
> The Thrall/Pulse interplay gives the scene its charge. Two people who professionally manufacture desire, negotiating in a haze of their own powers. Senna uses proximity to control; Dante uses it to read.

### What Dante Sees
Dante reads what Senna won't admit — the haze, the open drawer, the flush that won't recede. This isn't strategy. This is a woman being eaten alive by her own hunger. He almost tells her. Instead he lets his Pulse brush her one more time and leaves.
> Dante's near-warning is the chapter's outside perspective on Senna's descent. A professional manipulator recognizes someone past the point where plans work — and chooses not to say it. His complicity is transactional, not emotional.
"Tomorrow, then."

### Wrong Heat
The door closes. Senna stands in Dante's residual warmth and it's wrong — wrong temperature, wrong frequency. Not cold enough. Not Rei enough.
> Every heat source that isn't Rei registers as wrong. Senna's desire has become so specific that even another A-Class manipulator's warmth feels like static. She doesn't want heat. She wants frost that melts for her.

## The Prayer
Last thing before sleeping: the drawer again. The photograph. Her thumb traces Rei's jawline — the same path Kade's fingers took. Thrall blooms one final time, involuntary, unstoppable.
> The chapter ends where it began — at the drawer, reaching for Rei. The spiral is complete. Nothing resolved. Nothing released. Just a woman alone with her obsession, her power betraying her, and the words that used to be a war cry now broken into begging.
"She's mine." The words come out broken. Not a claim. Not anymore. A prayer to a god who's already chosen someone else.
