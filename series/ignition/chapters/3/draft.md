# Senna's Room

The mirror is fogged and Senna can't remember when that happened.

She's pacing. Uniform unbuttoned to the sternum, fabric sticking to damp skin, her heat-haze pressing against the walls of her quarters like something trying to escape. The room is sweltering. She's making it sweltering. She can't stop.

Can't stop seeing it.

His hands on Rei's face. The earring cracking — frost shattering off in tiny crystals while Rei's lips parted and her eyes went bright and her whole body leaned into a D-Class burnout like he was the first thing she'd ever wanted to touch.

Senna stops at the vanity. The drawer.

She opens it the way you open a wound — knowing it'll hurt, needing to see. Two years of Rei, curated. A silk scarf that still holds traces of frost in its weave. A pen Rei left in the study hall last semester, the cap bitten once — the only evidence that Rei Ashford has ever been anything less than perfect. A photograph from the rankings ceremony: Rei at the podium, silver hair catching light, face composed like marble. Senna is barely visible at the frame's edge. Watching. Always watching.

She brings the scarf to her face. Breathes in. Cold silk against her mouth, and underneath — faint, fading — the ghost of Rei's skin. That clean frost-and-nothing scent that Senna has been chasing for two years, pressing her nose to Rei's collar, her jaw, the hollow of her throat, trying to get close enough to taste what the cold is hiding.

Her fingers find the photograph. Heat bleeds from her fingertips, leaving marks on the glossy surface. She traces Rei's collarbones — slow, possessive, down — the way she's imagined doing it in person a thousand times. Down the sternum. Along the jaw. The path of a touch she's never been allowed to finish.

"Let me warm you up."

The collar touch replays. That morning — her thumb finding Rei's pulse through fabric, pressing just hard enough to feel it quicken. The two-year routine. Warmth offered, frost resisting, Senna reading that resistance as foreplay. She'd been so certain. Rei's cold always pushed back, but Senna thought pushing back was the game. Thought patience would crack what force couldn't. Thought if she kept her hands warm enough, kept pressing close enough, kept breathing against Rei's neck until the space between their skin tasted like want —

Now the reframing hits and it's poison.

Rei wasn't resisting to be conquered. Rei was resisting because Senna's touch didn't make her want to stop.

But his did.

The storage room replays. Each loop distorts further, memory dissolving into the thing Senna is most afraid of. Kade's hands on Rei's face become Kade's hands sliding into Rei's hair, tilting her head back, Rei's throat exposed and arching. The earring doesn't just crack — it shatters while Rei presses into him, while her cold breaks apart and her breath comes ragged and her fingers grip his shirt like she'll die if he steps back. Senna sees Rei's mouth opening. Sees Rei's eyes closing. Sees the face she's memorized for two years making an expression she's never once made for Senna.

That Rei *opened* for him. Opened the way Senna has spent two years trying to pry open — with patient fingers and warm breath and Thrall pressed against every inch of available skin and it was never enough, never, and he did it in thirty seconds with his bare hands on her face and a voice that said *there you are* like he'd found something Senna didn't know existed.

Thrall activates.

Not the controlled warmth she uses on others — the targeted, deliberate heat she presses into skin to make people lean closer, need more, come back. This is raw. Directionless. Want pooling low in her body like liquid heat, flushing up through her stomach, her chest, her throat. The shimmer-haze thickens until the air is wet. Her nipples are hard against the damp fabric of her shirt and she didn't decide that. Her body decided. Her body is reaching for Rei, and Rei isn't here.

She presses the scarf against her throat. Then lower. Cold silk against hot skin, and she imagines Rei's hands where the fabric touches — Rei's freezing fingers sliding down her sternum, frost blooming across Senna's flushed chest, the contrast so sharp it makes her gasp. The frost on the scarf melts against her skin. Two years of Rei's cold dissolving under ten seconds of Senna's need.

She imagines more. Rei underneath her, finally — ice cracking not from some burnout's clumsy hands but from Senna's mouth on her throat, Senna's Thrall sinking through skin, Senna's warmth flooding into all the places Rei keeps frozen until Rei can't tell the difference between warmth and want and she stops resisting and she *opens* —

Senna catches herself.

Scarf clutched against her bare chest. Breathing wrecked. Thrall radiating to an empty room like a body performing for no audience. She's wet and shaking and alone and this is what he reduced her to. Wanting in the dark. Touching fabric instead of skin.

She throws the scarf back. Slams the drawer. Won't finish. Won't give herself the release, because release means admitting the distance is real — that she's alone with this, that Rei isn't on the other side of it, that the only person Senna is making desperate is herself.

She buttons her shirt with hands that shake.

Jace. Think about Jace. The warning he delivered — burnout didn't flinch, laughed the same way he laughed at Senna in the storage room. But Jace was different when he came back. Flushed. Hands trembling. Rings clinking against each other like he couldn't keep still. She'd grabbed his wrist and asked what happened. He'd pulled away — pulled away from *Senna*, who he's never pulled away from — and said nothing.

She let him go because she needed the lie.

Now, alone, the doubt eats. What did Kade's heat do to her cousin? Why did Jace come back looking like he'd been touched by something he wanted?

Strategy. She needs strategy, not sensation.

Patience: failed. Jace's warning: failed. The storage room confrontation: failed. She threatened destruction and the burnout laughed in her face and said —

"She wouldn't choose the girl who makes her feel like a trophy."

Senna's nails bite into her palms. She needs to stop hearing his voice. She can't. He sounds like he knows something about Rei that Senna doesn't, and that's the part that makes her sick — not that he touched Rei, but that he *saw* her. That he looked at the ice queen and found the wanting girl underneath and Senna has been staring at that same ice for two years and never —

Dante.

The thought lands like a blade drawn. Last year she aimed Dante's Pulse near Rei. Manufactured attraction. Third-party seduction meant to trigger jealousy. Failed — Rei felt nothing then. But Rei feels things now. Kade cracked the seal. Which means Dante's Pulse could work differently. Not aimed at Rei. Aimed at the space around Kade. Make everyone want him. Flood him with manufactured desire until Rei sees him drowning in it and thinks: *he's not special. He's not mine. He's everyone's.*

She texts him. He arrives in minutes, because Dante always comes when Senna calls — the way pretty weapons come to the hand that wields them.

He leans in the doorway. Shirt open at the collar, dark hair falling just right, Pulse simmering under his skin like cologne. His eyes move over her — the rebuttoned shirt she missed a button on, the damp skin at her throat, the heat-haze filling the room, the vanity drawer she didn't close all the way. Dante has seen Senna hungry before. He's never seen her like this. He can smell obsession the way sharks smell blood.

"Late night," he says.

Senna pitches it standing too close to him, because proximity is how she negotiates. How she controls. Thrall leaking between them like foreplay — warm pressure against his chest, his jaw, the hollow of his throat. A D-Class burnout who touched what's hers. Needs to be made ordinary. Dante's Pulse aimed at the people around Kade — make him wanted, make him common, make what Rei felt look cheap.

Dante listens with his mouth curved and his eyes half-lidded and his Pulse brushing back against her Thrall in a way that makes the air between them shimmer and thicken. Two powers designed to make people want, pressing against each other in a room that already smells like desire. He doesn't ask why. He asks what's in it for him. Senna answers with a look that promises and threatens in equal measure, and Dante reads both fluently.

But Dante is a professional reader of want. He makes people fall. It's what he does. And what he reads in Senna right now — the haze, the open drawer, the flush that won't recede, the way she's standing too close because she needs to be close to *someone* and the person she needs isn't available — that's not strategy.

That's a woman being eaten alive by her own hunger.

He almost tells her. Almost says she's past the point where plans work. Instead he lets his Pulse brush her one more time — a professional courtesy, one manipulator recognizing another — and pushes off the doorframe.

"Tomorrow, then."

The door closes. Senna stands in the heat Dante left behind, and it's wrong. Wrong temperature. Wrong frequency. Not cold enough. Not Rei enough. Not anything.

The drawer. Again.

She can't not. The photograph. Rei's face, untouchable, perfectly composed — the jawline Senna has memorized with her eyes and never been allowed to memorize with her mouth. Her thumb traces it. The same path Kade's fingers took. The path that cracked the earring and the ice and the two years of certainty that Rei would eventually be hers.

Thrall blooms one final time. Involuntary. Unstoppable. Her body still reaching for someone who isn't reaching back.

"She's mine."

The words come out broken. Not a claim. Not anymore. A prayer to a god who's already chosen someone else.
