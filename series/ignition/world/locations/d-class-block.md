# D-Class Block

The Burn Ward — where the academy puts students it considers broken. Kade's territory. The scorch marks on the walls aren't damage, they're history — every spike, every loss of control, every student who felt too much and couldn't stop. The D-Class community bonds through shared marginalization, protecting their own from a system that labels them dangerous. Kade has been sent to the basement four times for uncontrolled spikes. He still lives here by choice. The warmth is real.

Utilitarian concrete dormitory for D-Class students. Heat-scarred walls from power surges. Warm amber lighting, industrial aesthetic. Deliberately uncomfortable but alive with energy. The architecture says: you're dangerous, but at least you're real.
