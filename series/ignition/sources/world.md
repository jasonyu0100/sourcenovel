# World — Ignition

## Apex Academy

Elite institution for training emotion-powered individuals. Founded sixty years ago when the Awakening made it clear that emotions could literally burn cities down if left unmanaged. The academy's mission: teach control. Its method: hierarchy.

### Location
Climate-controlled zone in the Pacific Northwest. Perpetual overcast provides natural temperature moderation. The campus is isolated — students live on-site, separated from the "normal" world for their protection and everyone else's.

### Architecture
Brutalist foundation with class-coded additions:

**S-Class Tower (The Spire)**
Glass and steel, climate-locked at 62°F. Minimalist interiors, individual rooms, observation-level windows. The message: you've risen above.

**A-Class Wing**
Modern, comfortable, aspirational. Shared suites, good amenities. The message: keep climbing.

**B-Class Hall**
Functional residential. Dorms, cafeteria access, standard facilities. The message: you're adequate.

**C-Class Commons**
Crowded but warm. Shared everything. Heavy empathic population. The message: you feel too much but we need you.

**D-Class Block (The Burn Ward)**
Concrete and scorch marks. Heat-resistant everything. Isolated from main campus. The message: you're dangerous.

### Key Locations

**The Ranking Board**
Public display in the main atrium. Updated every Friday. Your number determines your life. Climbing is possible; falling is devastating. Everyone checks it. Everyone pretends they don't care.

**Training Grounds**
Clinical white facilities with temperature monitors everywhere. Where students learn control through repeated suppression exercises. Cold showers. Meditation chambers. Endurance tests.

**The Cooling Room**
Sub-zero meditation space for S-Class. Mandatory daily attendance. Where Rei learned to feel nothing. The walls are frosted year-round.

**The Basement**
Where D-Class students go when they "spike" uncontrollably. Reinforced. Isolated. No one talks about what happens down there. Kade has been four times.

**The Neutral Zone**
Cross-class common spaces — cafeteria, library, outdoor grounds. Technically accessible to all. Socially segregated by unspoken rules.

## The Power System: Flame Class

### Origin
Forty years ago, a percentage of the population began manifesting emotion-linked abilities. The stronger the emotion, the stronger the effect. Trauma survivors, passionate artists, and the deeply feeling suddenly became dangerous.

Society's response: classify, rank, control.

### Manifestation
Powers typically emerge during puberty, tied to emotional development. First manifestation is usually uncontrolled — buildings burn, spaces freeze, empaths overwhelm hospitals. The academy exists to prevent second-incident casualties.

### Classification System

**S-Class (Supreme Control)**
Perfect suppression. Emotion exists but never manifests. Valued by corporations, government, military. Rei is rated S-1 — the highest score in a decade.

**A-Class (Advanced Control)**
Excellent suppression with occasional flickers under extreme stress. Still highly employable. Senna operates here — controlled but passionate.

**B-Class (Baseline Control)**
Average. Emotional reactions happen but are contained. The majority. Invisible.

**C-Class (Challenged Control)**
Frequent emotional leakage. Empaths, sensitives, those who feel others' feelings. Useful in therapy, interrogation, but pitied. Theo's empathy makes him C-Class despite decent control.

**D-Class (Dangerous/Deficient)**
Unable to suppress consistently. Labeled "burnouts." Tracked, monitored, marginalized. The system says they're broken. The truth: many have the most raw power. They just can't stop feeling.

### The Hidden Truth
Control isn't strength — it's suppression. The ranking system inverts natural power. D-Class students often have exponentially more potential than S-Class. They're just punished for accessing it.

The students who "lose control" together — who let themselves feel in proximity to someone else — create power feedback loops that dwarf anything achievable through suppression.

The academy knows this. They suppress it anyway.

## Social Structure

### Class Hierarchy
Your Flame Class determines:
- Where you live
- Who you eat with
- Who you can date
- Your career prospects
- Your social worth

### Cross-Class Contact
Officially permitted in neutral zones. Unofficially: S-Class doesn't associate with D-Class. A-Class aspires up, not down. The rules are unwritten but absolute.

### Forbidden Relationships
Cross-class romantic relationships are explicitly prohibited. Emotional entanglement between disparate classes creates "instability events" — power surges, control breaks, sometimes injuries. The policy is "for everyone's protection."

The enforcement is social: ostracization, ranking penalties, parental notification, career sabotage.

### The Ranking Game
Students constantly jockey for position. Maintaining control under stress earns points. Breaking control loses them. The system creates a culture of emotional repression masked as self-improvement.

## Faculty

### Professor Vance (Control Theory)
Former S-Class. Fell in love with a C-Class fifteen years ago. Their relationship became an "instability event" that hospitalized three bystanders. She left him after the scandal. He still wears the ring.

Now he teaches control to students making the same mistakes. He sees Rei and Kade circling each other. He knows how this story ends.

### Other Faculty
Career suppressors who succeeded by feeling nothing. They enforce hierarchy because they believe in it — or because they're afraid of what happens if it breaks.

## Cultural Context

### Public Perception
Powered individuals are valuable but frightening. Control is the price of acceptance. "Burnouts" are portrayed in media as ticking time bombs. S-Class are celebrities, executives, leaders.

### Student Culture
- S-Class: Isolated, envied, pressured
- Mid-ranks: Competitive, anxious, conformist
- D-Class: Defiant, bonded, treated as lost causes

### The Underground
Rumors of spaces where students meet without surveillance. Where control is optional. Where cross-class contact happens without consequence. No one admits they exist.
