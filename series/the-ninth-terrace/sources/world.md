# World Source — The Ninth Terrace

## The Forgetting

The moment humanity forgot about death. Not gradually — all at once. When the technology came that made mortality optional, civilization discovered it had been carrying two incompatible answers to the same question. Families fractured overnight. Institutions collapsed. What emerged were two Thrones built on irreconcilable visions of what humanity should become.

The Forgetting is recent — years, not generations. The ideological split hardened into borders, the borders into Thrones, the Thrones into rival superpowers. Everyone alive remembers the world before. Everyone chose.

Technology and AI enabled the Forgetting, but this is not a civilization of passive dependence. People are educated, capable, driven by ideals. They use their own heads. The question of mortality wasn't thrust upon them — they met it as a nation of thinkers and answered differently.

## The Two Thrones

### The Ascendancy (First Throne)
Those who chose to remember. The Ascendancy holds that mortality gives life meaning — that death is the frame, the weight, the pressure that makes every choice matter. Without finitude, they argue, existence becomes endless drift. They are not Luddites rejecting technology; they are philosophers choosing limitation. The Ascendancy controls the core systems — the oldest, most developed, most resource-rich sectors of human space. Its culture values tradition, continuity, and the weight of consequence.

- **Capital**: Meridian — a sprawling ecumenopolis planet, the administrative heart of human civilization
- **Military doctrine**: Fleet superiority, mecha divisions, Paragon-led strike groups
- **Culture**: Formal, hierarchical, tradition-bound. The weight of mortality made visible in every institution. Old money, old power, old names that will end.
- **Core belief**: Death is not the enemy. Forgetting death is.

### The Sovereignty (Second Throne)
Those who chose to forget. The Sovereignty holds that mortality was always the cage — that every human value was shaped by scarcity, fear, and the knowledge of ending. Remove the cage and humanity can finally become what it was meant to be. They are not hedonists escaping consequence; they are builders choosing transcendence. The Sovereignty controls the frontier and mid-rim systems — territories rich in raw resources and open to transformation.

- **Capital**: Vantage — a massive orbital station-city, built rather than inherited, embodying the principle that the future is constructed, not received
- **Military doctrine**: Asymmetric warfare, elite mecha squads, technological superiority
- **Culture**: Meritocratic, competitive, forward-looking. Existence as project rather than inheritance. New money, new ideas, sharp elbows.
- **Core belief**: Death was the problem. We solved it. Now we build.

### The Contested Frontier
The systems between the Thrones — border worlds, mining colonies, trade hubs. Neither Throne has clear control. Proxy conflicts, intelligence operations, and economic pressure define life here. The frontier is where the cold war turns hot, incident by incident.

## The Ninth Terrace

### Governance
The Terrace is governed by a Council of Nine — its most powerful and senior residents. Council seats are not elected but recognized; when a seat opens, the remaining members acknowledge the most qualified candidate. The Council enforces the Terrace's neutrality and sovereignty. Decisions require a majority of five.

### Geography
The Terrace is structured in ascending tiers:
- **Lower Terrace (Tiers 1-3)**: Markets, public spaces, visitor quarters. The most accessible level. Trade, entertainment, and first impressions.
- **Middle Terrace (Tiers 4-6)**: Residences, workshops, training facilities. Where most permanent residents live and work. Idris's establishment is here.
- **Upper Terrace (Tiers 7-9)**: Council chambers, restricted facilities, the most powerful residents. Access by invitation or recognition only.

### Time Dilation
One day outside the Terrace equals approximately one week inside. The dilation field is generated by ancient technology that predates the Thrones — its exact mechanism is not fully understood. The field boundary is visible as a faint shimmer. Crossing the boundary is disorienting; newcomers often experience temporal vertigo for the first few hours.

### Culture
Technology is not banned on the Terrace (unlike Skyfire Avenue), but ostentation is frowned upon. The culture values mastery — of craft, of Discipline, of self. Residents tend to be understated about their power. The Terrace has its own economy, its own social codes, and its own notion of status that has nothing to do with which Throne you came from.

## DriftNet
The interstellar virtual network connecting both Thrones and the Terrace. Used for communication, entertainment, competitive mecha simulation, and intelligence gathering. DriftNet mecha tournaments are massive spectator events, drawing viewers from across both Thrones. The network is jointly maintained but constantly contested — both Thrones run surveillance and counter-surveillance operations within it.

## Power System Details

### Discipline Categories
- **Elemental**: Fire, ice, lightning, gravity, magnetism, etc.
- **Spatial**: Teleportation, barriers, dimensional manipulation
- **Temporal**: Time perception, acceleration, localized time dilation (extremely rare)
- **Psychic**: Telepathy, empathy, telekinesis, illusion
- **Physical**: Enhancement, transformation, regeneration, sensory amplification
- **Abstract**: Probability manipulation, causality, entropy (rarest and most dangerous)

### Prism Integration
Prisms are integrated into mecha power cores and personal equipment. An Adept with a compatible Prism experiences amplified Discipline output and reduced strain. Incompatible Prisms are useless at best, dangerous at worst. Prism compatibility is tested through resonance — a compatible Prism will vibrate and glow in an Adept's presence.

### Mecha
Mecha range from mass-produced military units to custom-built elite machines. Elite mecha are fitted with Prism cores and tuned to their pilot's Discipline, allowing the pilot to channel their genetic abilities through the machine. A God-class pilot in a Prism-cored mecha is a strategic asset equivalent to a small fleet.

Mecha combat is also a competitive sport — DriftNet tournaments and live exhibitions draw enormous audiences. Pilot rankings (Emperor-class, Sovereign-class, God-class) are recognized across both Thrones.
