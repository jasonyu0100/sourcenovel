# Arc 1 Setting

## The Shared Quarters
Idris and Sera's private space — an officers' apartment in a military installation that serves the still-unified government. Functional but personalized. Dark furniture, warm lighting, a window overlooking the city below. Two people's lives layered into a single space — his books stacked on her desk, her jacket draped over his chair. The kind of lived-in domesticity that takes years to build.

The room is the arc's primary stage. It should feel warm and real in the opening, then increasingly claustrophobic as the night progresses. By the end, it should feel empty even before the second person leaves.

## Sensory Palette
- **Sound**: The hum of climate systems. Distant city noise. Broadcast alerts that start quiet and get louder. The silence between two people who've run out of arguments.
- **Light**: Warm interior lighting (amber, gold). Cool blue-white from screens and broadcasts. The shift from warm to cool as the night progresses mirrors the emotional arc.
- **Touch**: The physical ease of people who know each other's bodies. A hand on a shoulder. Fingers brushing. The absence of contact in the final scenes — hands that don't reach.
- **Space**: The room gets smaller as the argument grows. Distances between characters widen. The door becomes the most important object in the room.

## The City Outside
Glimpsed through windows, heard through walls. The capital is in controlled chaos — military convoys, broadcast announcements, people making choices. The wider world intrudes through screens and messages, but the arc stays inside the room. The political fracture is weather — it shapes everything but it's not the story. The story is the two people the weather is happening to.
