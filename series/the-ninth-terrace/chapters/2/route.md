# Chapter 2 Route

Sera adjusts the collar of Idris's uniform — fingers finding the Ascendancy rank insignia at his throat, straightening the fold where the extraction creased it. The gesture is automatic, unhesitating. Not the unconscious reaching of someone pretending they don't need to touch him — just the simple possessiveness of someone who hasn't learned to hide it yet.

Their shared quarters. His tactical projections spread across her desk, her jacket draped over the back of his chair. Warm amber lighting, the hum of climate systems, a window overlooking the capital's skyline. Two lives layered into a single room — the accumulated evidence of people who fit.

Post-mission wind-down. Sera reviews the operation in shorthand — his Discipline mapped the resonance field before anyone else registered the cascade, her Spatial corridor got the extraction team clear with margin to spare. Professional admiration that doesn't bother separating itself from personal. They are better together than apart, and everyone who's worked with them knows it.

Sera watches Idris move through the room — pouring drinks, pulling up the debrief notes, arguing a tactical point with warmth in his voice. This Idris: vital, present, engaged. He leans into the world instead of away from it. He still believes the system is worth fighting for. She doesn't know yet that this is something a person can lose.

A name surfaces — Torren, or someone like him. A colleague from their unit who filed transfer orders last week. Chose a side before there were officially sides to choose. Sera notes the gap in the rotation, the redistributed duties. Idris acknowledges it without weight: people make choices. The first crack in the foundation, treated like weather.

A small ritual — tea, or checking tomorrow's schedule, or the particular way he hands her a datapad with the screen already angled for her grip. The kind of gesture that takes years to develop and exists only between two people. Mundane domesticity as the thing about to be lost.

Through the window, the capital looks normal — city lights, transit lines, the distant glow of the Senate complex. But there are more military convoys than usual. A broadcast tower cycles through advisory channels. The outside world is pressing closer, not yet through the door. Sera registers it the way a strategist registers troop movements: noted, filed, not yet actionable.

Sera leans into Idris — his arm settles around her, his hand finding the curve of her waist without looking. The physical ease of people who know each other's bodies. No performance. No calculation. The warmth of contact that hasn't yet learned to cost anything. Present-day Sera would never allow herself this unguarded. This Sera doesn't know she'll need to guard against it.

Sera starts to say something. The colleague's departure. The dissolution vote. What *origin* means when you hold different commissions. She looks at Idris — still arguing the finer points of resonance extraction theory, still present, still warm — and the words stay where they are. Tonight, she chooses not to name what's coming. The room holds. The light stays warm.
