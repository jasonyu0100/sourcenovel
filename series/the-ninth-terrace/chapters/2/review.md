## Engagement Notes
**Temporal contrast delivers.** The reader who met present-day Idris (withdrawn, hiding, anonymous) now watches him argue tactics with warmth, hand someone a datapad angled for their grip, cross a room to hold someone without asking. The gap between these two men is the chapter's engine.

**Sera's interiority works.** Her observation is sharp but not clinical — she notices the way he moves, the datapad habit, the convoys outside, but the noticing comes from affection, not strategy. The younger voice is distinct from the present-day Vice-Commandant.

**The closing beat lands.** "No. Nothing important." — the reader knows it's the most important thing in the room. The parallel with chapter 1's "chose not to count the seconds" creates structural rhyme without being heavy-handed. Both chapters end with someone choosing to let the evening hold.

**Torren's departure threads cleanly.** Not dwelt on, not dramatized. "People make choices" — a line that will gain weight as the arc progresses.

**The *origin* passage is the sharpest paragraph.** "It had never mattered because the government was one thing, and they served one chain of command, and the quarters they shared didn't belong to a Throne — they belonged to a unit." The reader already knows how this ends.
