# Chapter 2 Concept

## Active Threads
- Idris and Sera's relationship — established as present-day guarded intimacy in chapter 1, now rewound to its peak
- The political fracture — dissolution vote approaching, colleagues already choosing sides
- Idris's hidden power — chapter 1 established the "retired shopkeeper" facade; now the reader sees him before he built it
- The collar motif — Sera straightened his bare mandarin collar in chapter 1; now she touches rank insignia

## Direction
First flashback chapter. Sera's POV. The reader drops into the past through a sensory echo — the same gesture (adjusting his collar) in a completely different context. The tone shifts from the guarded present to open warmth. This is Idris and Sera at their peak: partners in a mission, partners in life, still believing the world will hold together.

## Key Scene
Post-mission debrief in their shared officers' quarters. They've just returned from a joint operation — the professional seamlessness bleeds into personal intimacy. Sera watches an Idris who is still vital, still engaged, still wearing rank insignia. Their shared space — his books on her desk, her jacket on his chair — is the physical evidence of a life built together. The mission wind-down reveals how they complement each other: how they fight, how they decompress, how easily they exist in the same room.

## Thread
A colleague's departure. Someone they served with has already chosen a Throne and left. Mentioned in passing — not dwelt on. Sera notices the gap in the roster, the empty chair, the reassigned duties. The first crack. The reader knows what it means even if Sera doesn't let herself linger on it.

## Engagement
- Technique: Temporal contrast — every warm detail is weighted by chapter 1's present-day distance
- Tension: The colleague's departure as background fracture; the reader measuring young Idris against the withdrawn shopkeeper they already met
- Key Moment: Sera almost says something about the political situation, what it means for them. She decides not to. Tonight, she chooses silence — mirroring Idris choosing not to count the seconds in chapter 1.

## Scope
- Location: Shared officers' quarters, unified military installation
- Timeframe: Evening after a joint mission, hours before the political crisis accelerates
- Focus: Intimacy — domestic routine, professional partnership, the small rituals of two people who fit
- POV: Sera Kaine (younger, ~26, unarmored, open)
- Word count target: ~1,000-1,500 words
