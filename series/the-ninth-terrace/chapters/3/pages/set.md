# Chapter 3 Set Design

## Locations

### Shared Quarters
Base: Officers' quarters in a military installation. Dark furniture, warm amber lighting, window overlooking the city. Two lives layered together — his books on her desk, her jacket on his chair. Couch area, kitchen alcove, wall-mounted screen. The domestic intimacy of a built life.

#### Angle Variations (generate as refs)
1. **quarters-wide-couch**
   Wide shot of couch area, amber lighting, books and personal effects visible

2. **quarters-door-interior**
   View from inside toward door, weapon rack visible, corridor light spilling in

3. **quarters-window**
   Window frame composition, city lights visible below, glass surface for reflections

### Corridor
Base: Military installation corridor. Functional, angular. Cool lighting contrasting with warm quarters. Clean lines, minimal decoration.

## Persistent Elements

### Lighting Signature
- Warm: Amber interior glow from quarters lighting, feels like safety
- Cool: Corridor light, starlight through window, feels like exposure
- Split: Warm interior vs. cool outside — the divide becoming visible

### Atmosphere
- Climate system hum (ambient, becomes audible in silence)
- Notification tones (priority military double-pulse)
- Three meters of distance that becomes a boundary
- Books and jacket as symbols of shared life

### Sound Cues (for later video)
- Door knock (sharp, breaking silence)
- Climate system hum
- Notification tone (double-pulse priority military)
- Convoy running lights (low mechanical hum)
- Silence (weighted, different from before)

## Visual Motifs

### Three Meters
The space between Idris and Sera that grows throughout Scene 2. Wide shots should show this distance. The gap that was nothing becomes the beginning of something larger.

### Face Down Datapad
The recall orders Idris won't open. Positioned between them on the table. Evidence. Confession. The decision he's avoided.

### Glass/Reflection
Sera's reflection in the window — motionless, watching. The separation between interior warmth and exterior cold made visible.

### Warm Lighting Turning Cold
The same amber lighting that was warm an hour ago now feels like exposure. Visual shift from comfort to tension without changing the actual light sources.
