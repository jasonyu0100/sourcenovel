# Chapter 3 Concept

## Active Threads
- The flashback evening continues — Sera and Idris in shared quarters
- Torren's departure established the pattern; now it accelerates
- The word "origin" hanging over everything — commissions, birth, loyalty
- Idris's refusal to engage with the crisis (present-day established this as his pattern)

## Direction
A fellow Adept arrives at their quarters — someone they know, making a choice and wanting them to follow. The visit collapses the timeline: choose now, tonight. Idris's POV shows the fracture through a man who has been refusing to look at it.

## Key Scene
The visitor forces acknowledgment that the time for not-choosing has ended. The evening's warmth from Chapter 2 is shattered by someone standing in their doorway with an ultimatum or a plea. Idris must respond — not to abstract politics, but to a face, a voice, a person who has already decided.

## Thread
The fracture moves from background to foreground. The pattern Sera noticed (Torren, the convoys, the broadcasts) now enters their physical space. Idris's avoidance strategy — cataloguing everything, choosing nothing — fails when the crisis has a face.

## Engagement
- Technique: The visitor as a mirror — someone who has chosen what Idris refuses to choose, showing him what decision looks like
- Tension: The timeline collapse — no more "later," no more waiting
- Key Moment: Idris seeing through his Discipline what the visitor feels — reading their heartbeat, their resonance, the truth under their words — and having to respond anyway

## Scope
- Location: Shared quarters (the space they built together, now invaded)
- Timeframe: Same evening as Chapter 2, later — the crisis arrives before dawn
- Focus: Confrontation — dialogue-heavy, the argument begins here
