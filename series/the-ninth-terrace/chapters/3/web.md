# Overview
A fellow Adept arrives at their quarters with news that collapses the timeline — the Senate has dissolved, recalls are immediate, and everyone must choose tonight. Idris's avoidance strategy fails when the crisis has a face.
> The chapter accelerates from warmth to tension as the political fracture physically enters the space Idris and Sera have built together. Every detail Idris perceives through his Discipline — heartbeats, signatures, distances — becomes evidence of a world splitting apart.

## The Knock
Idris senses Maren Caul's approach before she knocks — her resonance signature familiar but running hot with fear. Sera is still beside him on the couch, datapad dark. The evening they've been protecting is about to end.
> Idris's Discipline never turns off. He knows what's coming before it arrives, but knowing doesn't prepare him. The contrast with the protected evening — where they didn't talk about Torren or origin — makes the intrusion sharper.
"The evening they'd been protecting — the one where they didn't talk about Torren or the convoys or the word *origin* — was about to end."

### Reading Maren
Idris catalogues Maren before she speaks: heartbeat at ninety-two, Discipline partially manifested, the tension of someone who has already chosen and is terrified of it.
> His perception gives him access to truths people don't verbalize. Maren's fear isn't about the news — it's about what comes after delivering it.
"Maren's heartbeat at ninety-two, her Discipline partially manifested without her realizing it, the particular tension in her shoulders that said she'd already made a choice and was terrified of what came next."

### The Threshold
Maren stands in the doorway in field dress, no rank insignia visible. She doesn't apologize for the hour, doesn't make social sounds. She positions herself on the threshold as if coming fully inside would mean something different.
> The absent insignia and the threshold positioning both signal that normal rules have ended. Maren is already operating in the new reality; she's just come to tell them about it.

## The News
The Senate dissolved an hour ago. Both Thrones are recalling assets by origin. Third Fleet declared for the Ascendancy. The Sovereignty is processing voluntary transfers; the Ascendancy is recalling by birth registry.
> The political fracture reduces to a single operational fact: where you were born determines where you belong. Sera is Sovereignty-born. Idris is not.
"If you were born in their space, you're being recalled. Not requested. Recalled."

### The Pattern Becomes a Flood
Torren is already on a transport. Vasquez and Chen went with him. Orin-Daal was detained — wrong birth certificate.
> The pattern Sera noticed in Chapter 2 — and Idris dismissed — has become undeniable. People they served with are choosing, being chosen for, or being detained.
"The pattern Sera had noticed — the one Idris had dismissed with *people make choices* — had become a flood. And it was pouring through their doorway."

## The Offer
Maren is leaving tonight. A transport at the secondary port, no manifest filing. Room for more. Her eyes move to Idris and stay there — she wants him to come with them.
> Both sides will want Idris. His value is strategic. But Maren's plea isn't strategic — she wants someone she trusts on her side when everything falls apart.
"Both of you, if you want."

### The Non-Answer
Idris says he hasn't decided. The words sound hollow even to him — the same deflection he's used about everything. He catches Maren's heartbeat dropping into resignation.
> "People make choices" was his dismissal of Torren's departure. Now someone stands before him who has made one, and he's still pretending he can wait.
"The words came out flat. Even he could hear how hollow they sounded — the same deflection he'd used about Torren, about the convoys, about everything he didn't want to look at."

### The Exchange Between Women
Something passes between Maren and Sera that Idris can read but not fully interpret. A question about him. An answer about time.
> Even his Discipline has limits. He can read heartbeats but not the silent communication between two people who understand something about him that he doesn't.

## The Departure
Maren leaves without asking again. She looks at the quarters she's visited a hundred times — the books, the jacket, the life that was about to stop existing — and then she's gone.
> The room itself becomes evidence of what's ending. Every domestic detail Maren sees is a thing that won't exist in the same way after tonight.
"She just stood there for a moment longer, looking at the quarters she'd visited a hundred times — the books stacked on Sera's desk, the jacket draped over Idris's chair, the life they'd built that was about to stop existing."

### The Weighted Silence
The silence after the door closes is different — it has weight now, edges. Somewhere in the building, a priority military notification chimes. Someone else's life is about to change.
> The hum of the climate system, the notification tone — the background sounds that were ignorable are now punctuation marks in a conversation that hasn't started.
"The silence that followed was different from the silence before. It had weight now. Edges."

## The Datapad
Idris reaches for the datapad he's been ignoring all night — his recall orders. He still doesn't open it. He sets it on the table between them, face down, like evidence.
> The datapad has been present since Chapter 2, unopened, a symbol of his avoidance. Placing it on the table is the closest he comes to acknowledgment — not opening it, but no longer hiding it.
"He still didn't open it. He set it on the table between them instead, face down, like evidence. Like a confession."

## Three Meters
Idris measures the distance between them without meaning to. Three meters. An hour ago, that distance was nothing. Now it feels like the beginning of something much larger.
> His Discipline catalogs everything, including the growing space between him and Sera. The room hasn't changed, but it doesn't feel like theirs anymore.
"Idris found himself measuring the distance between them. Three meters. A little more."

### "Us"
Sera says Maren wanted him to come with *us*. The word lands harder than anything Maren said.
> Sera has already placed herself on one side of the fracture. "Us" means the Sovereignty. It means the side Idris isn't from. It means she's already thinking in terms of separation.
"*Us.* The word landed harder than anything Maren had said."

### The Anchoring Gesture
Sera's fingers press flat against the window glass, anchoring herself. Idris recognizes the gesture — he's seen it before difficult briefings, before impossible missions.
> The same gesture from Chapter 1, when present-day Sera anchored herself against stone. It's the physical cost of composure, the way she holds herself together through discipline rather than calm.
"Her fingers pressed flat against the glass, anchoring herself. Idris recognized the gesture."

### The Conversation That Should Be
The silence isn't warm anymore. It's the space where a conversation should be — the conversation neither of them knows how to start.
> The room that held easy intimacy in Chapter 2 now holds the absence of words. They both know what the datapad says. They both know what "origin" means. Neither can begin.
"They both knew what it said."
