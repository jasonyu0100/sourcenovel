# Chapter 6 Concept

## Active Threads
- Sera has walked out, leaving Idris in quarters that used to be theirs
- The Sovereignty is processing transfers; the fracture is hours old
- Sera is traveling toward a new life, armor going on in real time
- The political split is still raw — loyalties forming, institutions fracturing

## Direction
Sera's POV on a transport to Sovereignty space. The immediate aftermath of her departure — processing what she lost while moving toward what she chose. Heavy worldbuilding: futuristic space travel, the infrastructure of interstellar civilization, and the existential divide at the heart of the Forgetting. A conversation with a new character introduces context through dialogue and plants a seed for future arcs.

## Key Scene
Sera meets someone on the transport who will matter later — a future ally, rival, or complication in her Sovereignty career. Their conversation externalizes the existential conflict: what it means to choose to forget death, what it means to choose to remember it, why Idris couldn't follow her and why she couldn't stay. Through this dialogue, the reader learns that the Throne split isn't political — it's humanity dividing over the oldest question. And Sera chose her answer.

## Thread
The transformation begins here. The open, laughing Sera from Chapters 2-4 starts becoming the armored Vice-Commandant from Chapter 1. Every moment of vulnerability is met with a choice to harden. The new character sees something in her — potential, danger, or both.

## Engagement
- Technique: Worldbuilding through character perspective — Sera observes the space setting, the technology, the scale of the civilization, while filtering it through her emotional state
- Tension: She's raw from leaving Idris, but must present composure to strangers. The gap between what she feels and what she shows.
- Key Moment: The new character says something that lands — about the conflict, about Idris, about what the Sovereignty needs. It crystallizes something for Sera.

## Scope
- Location: Sovereignty military transport, interstellar travel, glimpses of fleet/station infrastructure
- Timeframe: Hours after Chapter 5. The transport journey.
- Focus: Dialogue + worldbuilding. Internal processing through external observation.

## Worldbuilding Goals
- The scale of interstellar civilization (light-years, fleet movements, station networks)
- **The Forgetting**: The moment humanity forgot about death. Technology made mortality optional, and civilization split along that fault line. The Ascendancy chose to remember death as meaning. The Sovereignty chose to forget — to build past the old limits.
- Why the split happened NOW: This is recent, not ancient history. Everyone alive remembers the world before.
- Technology texture: FTL travel, communications, Discipline integration with tech — advanced but people remain educated, independent thinkers
- The military context: ranks, structure, what Sera is joining — a Throne built on transcendence
