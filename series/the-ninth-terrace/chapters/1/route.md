# Chapter 1 Route

The corridor outside their quarters smelled like recycled air and antiseptic. Idris walked two steps behind Sera, watching the way she rolled her left shoulder — a habit she didn't know she had, working out the tension from the extraction. The mission was clean. They'd gotten the Prism out before the site collapsed. She was already thinking about something else.

Their door opened to warm light and the particular quiet of a space that belonged to two people. Idris noticed the things he always noticed — her jacket on his chair, his datapad balanced on a stack of her tactical briefs. The geography of a shared life, unremarkable and irreplaceable.

Sera moved through the space without ceremony — boots off, weapon holstered on the rack, hair pulled free. She said something about the extraction timing, about how the resonance field had almost destabilized, and Idris listened with the specific attention of someone who was also watching the way lamplight caught in her hair. He answered. She argued a point. He conceded. The rhythm was practiced, easy, the verbal equivalent of muscle memory.

A broadcast notification chimed — third one tonight. Sera glanced at the wall screen, and Idris saw the shift: the briefest tightening around her eyes before she muted it. Political briefing. Sovereignty faction leadership had released a statement. Neither of them reached for the details. The world outside could wait.

Idris stood in the kitchen alcove, doing something ordinary — preparing tea, or checking supplies, something with his hands. Sera leaned against the counter nearby, close enough to touch but not touching. They talked about nothing important. The mission. A Prism she wanted to study. A colleague's promotion that was political. The conversation had the texture of people who were happy and didn't need to announce it.

The moment: Sera reached over and straightened his collar — a small, automatic gesture, the kind of thing you do for someone without thinking about it. Idris caught her hand. Held it against his chest for a beat. She looked at him, and for a second neither of them was performing composure. Just two people in a warm room, at the end of a day that could have killed them, alive and together and not yet aware that this was the last time the world would let them be simple.

The chapter closes on Idris alone for a moment — Sera in the other room, the muted broadcast screen pulsing with an unread alert. He looked at it. He didn't open it. The warm light held. The quiet held. Tomorrow would come regardless.
