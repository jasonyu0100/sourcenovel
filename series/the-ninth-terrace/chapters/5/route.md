# Chapter 5 Route: The Interception

Idris stands alone in the quarters that used to be theirs. The door has just closed behind Sera — quiet, final. Her jacket still draped over his chair. Her scent still in the air. The datapad face-down on the table, still unopened.

He forces himself not to count the seconds since she left. Counting would make them real. Instead he focuses on the silence, the absence, the shape of the space where someone was.

His Discipline registers another presence approaching — a resonance signature he recognizes instantly, though he hasn't felt it in years. Valeria Caine. His pulse stays steady, but something in his chest tightens.

The knock comes. Unhurried, confident. Valeria doesn't wait for an answer — she never did. She enters wearing Ascendancy dress uniform, her dark hair swept back, her smile carrying the warmth of someone who already knows she's won something.

She surveys the room — the books, the jacket, the face-down datapad. Her smile doesn't change, but something in her eyes sharpens. She says she's here on official business, but they both know the Ascendancy doesn't send Valeria Caine for paperwork.

[PARALLEL: Sera walks through the corridor toward the secondary port. Her pace is measured, deliberate. She doesn't look back. The convoys are moving outside, and she's moving with them.]

Valeria closes the distance between them — not aggressive, just natural, the way she always moved around him. She says she heard about his synergy partner. She says the Ascendancy doesn't care about personal complications. She says they just want him home.

Idris reads her heartbeat: eighty-four, controlled but elevated. She's not afraid. She's anticipating. He asks how long she was waiting outside. She admits she heard the argument. She admits she waited for the door to close.

She reaches for his collar — the gesture Sera made a thousand times, now performed by someone else. Her fingers brush the fabric where the rank insignia used to be. She says she misses who he was. She says he doesn't have to be the man who stands alone in an empty room.

[PARALLEL: Sera reaches the transport dock. Maren is there, boarding. Their eyes meet across the platform. Maren's expression carries relief, question, grief. Sera gives her nothing.]

Valeria moves closer. Her hand stays on his collar. She tells him the Sovereignty will break Sera the way it breaks everyone — ambition dressed as conviction. She tells him he can come back to something real. Someone who wants him without conditions.

Idris feels the pull — not of desire, but of exhaustion. The temptation of someone easier. Someone who doesn't ask him to choose between love and conviction. Valeria has always known how to offer exactly what the wounded part of him wants.

She leans in. Close enough that he can feel her breath. The almost-kiss hangs between them — a question neither of them has answered yet. Valeria waits, patient, certain. She's done this before. She knows how it ends.

Idris catches her wrist. Gently, but final. He moves her hand away from his collar. He tells her that Sera just walked through that door — and everything that matters walked out with her. He can't want someone easier. He doesn't know how.

Valeria's composure cracks for the first time — not anger, something sadder. She says he'll regret this when Sera forgets him. She says she'll be waiting when he changes his mind. She straightens her uniform and walks to the door, pausing at the threshold.

[PARALLEL: Sera boards the transport. The door seals behind her. She finds a seat by the window and watches the lights of the capital shrink as the ship lifts. She doesn't cry. She just watches until there's nothing left to see.]

The door closes behind Valeria — the same quiet click that ended everything with Sera. Idris stands alone again. His books on her desk. Her jacket on his chair. The datapad still face-down, still unopened. The room holds the shapes of everyone who's left.

He finally picks up the datapad. His recall orders. The words he's been avoiding all night. He reads them once, then sets the device down again. The choice was never about the orders. It was about whether he could become someone who waits while the world burns. He couldn't ask Sera to be that person. He can't be that person either.

Outside, the convoys keep moving. The fracture keeps happening. Somewhere above him, a transport clears the atmosphere carrying the woman he loves toward a future he won't be part of. And Idris stands alone in the quarters that used to be theirs, finally counting the seconds, because not counting them didn't make them any less real.
