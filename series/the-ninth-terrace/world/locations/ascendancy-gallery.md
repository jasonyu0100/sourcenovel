# The Living Gallery

History rendered in light and living stone. The Ascendancy's answer to museums — except nothing here is dead. The walls are carved from a mineral that responds to proximity, surfaces brightening as you approach and dimming as you pass. Relief panels depict the Founding Wars, the First Accord, the lineages of every major house — scenes the Sovereignty's official histories have conveniently omitted. Crystal formations grow from the ceiling in slow geological time, refracting light into shifting prismatic patterns across the floor. The Sovereignty calls this propaganda. The Ascendancy calls it memory. The gallery runs the full length of the flagship's inner hull, and walking it takes the better part of an hour. Every Ascendancy child walks it before their tenth year. Every Sovereignty diplomat avoids it.

A long cathedral-like gallery inside an ancient spacecraft. Walls of luminous carved stone with detailed relief panels depicting historical battle and ceremonial scenes. Crystal formations growing from the vaulted ceiling, casting prismatic rainbow light patterns on the floor. Warm stone tones — amber, ochre, deep rose — with scattered prismatic highlights. Carved archways receding into the distance creating dramatic perspective. Living stone texture that appears to glow from within. Floor of polished dark stone reflecting the crystal light. The sense of immense age and careful preservation.
