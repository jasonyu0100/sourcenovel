# Council Chambers

The seat of the Ninth Terrace's governance — where the Council of Nine convenes to decide the station's fate. Nine seats arranged in a crescent facing a central dais, each occupied by one of the most powerful Adepts in known space. The chamber sits near the apex of the Terrace's tiered architecture, high enough that the viewport behind the crescent shows nothing but stars and the distant thread of a shipping lane. This is where the Terrace's neutrality is defended, negotiated, and sometimes sold. The Senatorial Council has voted to formally dissolve — both Thrones are recalling their Adepts, envoys arrive tomorrow. The Council of Nine must decide whether to comply, resist, or play for time.

The chamber is austere by the Terrace's standards — dark stone walls polished to a mirror finish, the crescent of high-backed chairs carved from the same material as the Terrace's foundation. No ornamentation except the nine sigils above each seat, representing the nine tiers of Discipline mastery. The floor is a single piece of dark crystal that reflects the starfield from the viewport, creating the illusion that the council sits suspended in the void. Ambient lighting comes from recessed strips in the ceiling — cool blue-white, the only space on the Terrace that feels deliberately cold. A holographic projection array occupies the central dais, capable of displaying star charts, diplomatic communications, or evidence. The acoustics are perfect — a whisper from any seat reaches every other.
