# Envoy Wing

The diplomatic quarter — a carefully neutral corridor of suites reserved for representatives of both Thrones. The architecture is deliberately balanced: identical suites on opposite sides of a central atrium, mirror images down to the placement of furniture. Neither Throne's aesthetic dominates. This is where the formal dissolution plays out — envoys arriving with recall orders, diplomats calculating the cost of the Terrace's neutrality, intelligence officers mapping the station's defences behind polite smiles. The Wing is the most watched space on the Terrace. Every meeting here is observed, analysed, and reported.

A long central atrium with a vaulted ceiling of frosted glass that diffuses artificial daylight evenly. The floor is pale stone — a deliberate contrast to the Terrace's typical dark aesthetic, designed to feel exposed rather than intimate. Diplomatic suites line both sides — heavy doors marked with temporary Throne insignia (Ascendancy gold on the left, Sovereignty silver on the right). Between them, the atrium offers formal meeting spaces: round tables on raised platforms, each with its own sound-dampening field indicated by a faint shimmer. Security is invisible but omnipresent — the walls are reinforced, the sightlines are calculated, and the ambient energy signature is monitored constantly. Small diplomatic gardens punctuate the corridor — contained ecosystems of plants from different settled worlds, a gesture toward the diversity of the civilization they're all fighting over.
