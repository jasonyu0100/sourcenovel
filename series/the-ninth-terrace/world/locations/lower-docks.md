# Lower Docks

The working port beneath the Terrace's polished surface — where supplies arrive, refugees are processed, and the station's infrastructure meets the void. Unlike the Transport Dock's military precision, the Lower Docks are civilian chaos: cargo haulers jockeying for berth assignments, traders arguing customs fees, and the occasional unauthorized arrival slipping through during shift changes. This is where the Terrace's neutrality is tested daily — smugglers running Prisms between Thrones, deserters seeking sanctuary, and intelligence operatives arriving under commercial cover. The dock master's office has a window that faces the void and a cabinet with bottles from twelve systems.

A cavernous industrial space carved into the Terrace's lowest tier. The ceiling is high enough to accommodate medium-class freighters, with crane arms and magnetic grapples hanging from tracked rails above. The floor is scarred metal grating over maintenance corridors. Loading bays line the walls — massive doors that open to the void, sealed by atmospheric barriers that shimmer blue when ships pass through. Cargo containers are stacked in organised chaos, marked with Throne-coded shipping labels. The lighting is harsh fluorescent in the working areas, warmer in the small offices and break rooms clustered along the inner wall. A constant background hum of ventilation systems, engine cooling, and comm chatter. Fuel vapour and recycled air give the space its particular smell.
