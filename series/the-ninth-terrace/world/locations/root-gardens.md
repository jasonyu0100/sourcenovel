# The Root Gardens

The Ascendancy's oldest living argument. A terraced botanical garden deep in the flagship's core — plants that have been cultivated continuously for ten thousand years, species that went extinct everywhere else during the Forgetting but survived here because the Ascendancy never stopped tending them. Bioluminescent mosses carpet the walkways in soft blue-green light. Trees with bark like polished copper grow from soil beds integrated into the ship's hull, their roots threaded through the vessel's superstructure so thoroughly that removing them would compromise the ship. The Ascendancy chose this metaphor deliberately: the old growth and the vessel are one organism now. The air is thick and humid, fragrant with pollen from flowers that bloom on cycles longer than human lifetimes. Sovereignty botanists have formally requested seed samples seven times. They've been refused seven times.

A terraced botanical garden deep inside an ancient spacecraft. Multiple levels of lush vegetation growing from soil beds integrated into the ship's curved hull. Bioluminescent blue-green mosses carpeting stone walkways. Trees with copper-coloured bark growing upward through openings in the decks above, roots visibly threaded through the hull superstructure. Warm amber light filtering through translucent ceiling panels. Hanging vines and exotic flowers in deep purples and golds. Humid atmosphere with visible mist. Ancient carved stone irrigation channels. The feeling of a primeval forest transplanted into a living ship.
