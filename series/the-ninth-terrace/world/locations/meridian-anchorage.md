# Meridian Anchorage

The Ascendancy's answer to Vantage Station — except Meridian wasn't built. It was inherited. An orbital anchorage around the oldest continuously inhabited world in known space, where the Ascendancy's fleet has docked for ten thousand years. The docking spires are carved from asteroid stone and threaded with the same crystal-veined mineral that runs through the flagship. Ancient vessels hang in formation around the anchorage like ornaments on a chandelier — some operational, some preserved as monuments, all maintained with the same obsessive care. Through the anchorage viewports, Meridian itself is visible: a planet whose surface lights trace city grids laid down before the Forgetting. Where the Sovereignty sees Vantage's unfinished construction cranes and calls it potential, the Ascendancy looks at Meridian's ten-thousand-year skyline and calls it proof. Both are right. That's the problem.
