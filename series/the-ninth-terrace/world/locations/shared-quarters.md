# Shared Quarters

Where Idris and Sera built a life before the world broke. The last evening before the choosing — they wind down from a joint extraction, still wearing insignia that hasn't yet become a dividing line. His books on her desk, her jacket on his chair. Years of domesticity layered into a single space. A colleague has quietly departed — the first crack in the foundation they dismiss as weather. By morning the Senate will dissolve, both Thrones will begin recalling assets, and three meters of this room will become an unbridgeable distance. But tonight the kettle is on and the amber light holds.

Officers' quarters in a military installation. Functional but personalized — dark furniture, warm amber lighting, a window overlooking the city below. Two people's lives layered into a single space: his books on her desk, her jacket on his chair, a glass left on the counter. A small kitchen alcove with an electric kettle. Wall-mounted screen for broadcasts. Weapon rack by the door. The kind of lived-in domesticity that takes years to build.
