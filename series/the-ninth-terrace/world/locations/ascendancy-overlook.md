# Ascendancy Overlook

Where tradition meets the void. The highest accessible point on the Ascendancy flagship — an observation platform enclosed in a dome of crystal-veined stone that's been polished translucent over millennia of careful maintenance. Through it, the Ascendancy fleet is visible in formation: vessels whose hull designs haven't changed in ten thousand years, because the Ascendancy believes some things were right the first time. Carved stone benches ring the outer edge. The floor is a mosaic of the star charts that guided the original fleet. Standing here, you understand what the Ascendancy is selling — continuity, legitimacy, the argument that the future belongs to those who remember the past. The Sovereignty built Vantage Station to make the same argument in reverse.

A circular observation platform at the top of an ancient spacecraft. Dome of translucent crystal-veined stone overhead showing stars and a fleet of ornate ancient vessels in formation beyond. Carved stone benches ringing the outer edge. Floor mosaic of star charts in gold and deep blue inlay. Warm amber uplighting from recessed channels in the stone. The fleet visible through the translucent dome — organic curved hull designs with carved relief surfaces, arranged in ceremonial formation. A sense of height, openness, and ancient grandeur. Void and stars visible in all directions through the semi-transparent dome.
