# Ascendancy Pavilion

An ornate reception hall aboard the Ascendancy flagship — the first thing visitors see when they cross from the Terrace's lower docks via shuttle to berth seven. The Ascendancy doesn't do subtle. Where the Sovereignty strips everything to function, the Ascendancy layers history into every surface. Gilded arches frame a vaulted ceiling painted with scenes from the Founding — ten thousand years of inherited purpose rendered in luminescent pigment that shifts with the viewing angle. Living vines thread through carved stone columns, fed by hydroponic channels hidden in the architecture. The air carries the scent of old incense and something botanical, sweet and unfamiliar. Delegates from the Ninth Terrace are received here, offered tea from cups older than most civilisations, and reminded — without a word being spoken — what the Ascendancy believes it is owed.

A grand reception hall inside an ancient spacecraft. Vaulted ceiling with gilded arches and luminescent murals depicting historical scenes. Carved stone columns wrapped with living green vines. Warm amber and gold lighting from recessed fixtures and bioluminescent plant life. Ornate floor tiles in geometric patterns, deep reds and golds. Low ceremonial furniture — curved benches, a central tea table. Incense smoke drifting in thin wisps. A large viewport at the far end showing stars. The aesthetic is ancient temple meets living spacecraft — organic curves, carved relief panels, nothing sharp or industrial.
