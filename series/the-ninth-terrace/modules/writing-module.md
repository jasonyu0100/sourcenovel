# Writing Module — The Ninth Terrace

## Voice
Third person limited, alternating between Idris and Sera. Each chapter follows one POV. The narrative voice is close — we're inside their heads, feeling what they feel, but the prose maintains composure. The characters are controlled people; the writing mirrors that control while letting cracks show through observation and subtext.

## Style

### Prose Character
Cool, precise, and elegant. Sentences are clean — no purple prose, no overwrought metaphor. Beauty comes from restraint and rhythm. When the prose does reach for something lyrical, it earns it through contrast with the surrounding control.

### Sentence Structure
Varied. Short declarative sentences for impact and revelation. Longer, flowing sentences for atmosphere, setting, and internal observation. The rhythm shifts with emotional intensity — tighter and more clipped under tension, more expansive in quiet moments.

### Dialogue
Sharp, economical. Characters say less than they mean. Subtext carries the weight. Sera speaks with precision — every word chosen, every pause deliberate. Idris speaks simply, almost casually, in a way that makes people underestimate him. What they don't say to each other matters more than what they do.

### Interior Thought
Woven into action and observation, not set apart. Idris notices things — details, patterns, the way someone shifts their weight. His interior life is expressed through what he pays attention to. Sera's interior life is expressed through the gap between her public composure and private calculation.

## Vocabulary

### Register
Elevated but accessible. No jargon for jargon's sake. Technical terms (Disciplines, Tiers, Paragons, Prisms) are used naturally, as part of the characters' lived reality. The prose never stops to explain what a character would already know.

### Series-Specific Terminology
- **Disciplines** — always capitalized, treated as proper nouns
- **Adept** — capitalized when used as a title or rank
- **Tiers** — "Tier 6," "Tier 9," always with numeral
- **Paragon** — capitalized, used as both rank and noun
- **Quintessence** — capitalized, the transcendent force
- **Paragon stages** — Threshold, Convergence, Singularity, Absolute
- **The Thrones** — always capitalized, refers to the two superpowers
- **The Terrace** — shorthand for the Ninth Terrace, capitalized
- **Prisms** — capitalized, graded S/A/B/C
- **Mecha ranks** — Emperor-class, Sovereign-class, God-class
- **DriftNet** — the interstellar virtual network

### Words to Favor
precise, measured, still, weight, edge, quiet, clean, deliberate, sharp, cool, aware, composed

### Words to Avoid
suddenly, very, really, amazing, awesome, incredible, literally, basically, simply

## Structure

### Chapter Format
- ~1,000-1,500 words per chapter
- Single POV per chapter, alternating Idris and Sera
- Each chapter has a clear emotional beat and narrative movement
- Chapters end on hooks — questions raised, tensions unresolved, revelations half-glimpsed
- No chapter titles — numbered only

### Scene Construction
- Open with grounding — where, when, who, what they're doing
- Build through observation and interaction
- Close on a turn — something shifts, something is revealed, something is felt
- Avoid flashbacks in early chapters; establish the present before exploring the past

### Pacing
Measured and deliberate in quiet scenes. Accelerating in tension. Combat and confrontation use shorter paragraphs and faster cuts. Romance scenes slow down — the prose lingers where the characters want to linger.

## Lens
The story is seen through the eyes of two people who are very good at reading situations and controlling themselves. Idris sees the world with the awareness of someone who could destroy most of what's in front of him — his perspective carries a quiet weight, a constant background calculus of power dynamics he pretends not to notice. Sera sees the world as a board — people are pieces, conversations are moves, and she's always three steps ahead. Except with Idris, where she's exactly zero steps ahead and furious about it.

## Emotional Range
The writing should move between:
- **Cool observation** — the default register. Sharp, aware, controlled.
- **Quiet devastation** — when the control slips. Not melodrama — a single detail that lands like a punch.
- **Dry humor** — both characters are sharp and occasionally cutting. Humor is understated, never broad.
- **Tension** — political, romantic, combative. The prose tightens. Sentences shorten.
- **Awe** — for moments of genuine power, beauty, or scale. Rare, earned, affecting.
