# Scenario: Tensions Rising

## Setting
The morning after Chapter 1. The Ninth Terrace — humanity's last neutral ground — wakes to a new reality. The Senatorial Council has voted for formal dissolution. Both Thrones are recalling their Adepts. Envoys arrive today.

## What's Happening
The Sovereignty's Vice-Commandant Sera Kaine has arrived at the Command Spire with recall orders. Commandant Reva Orin, her superior, lands at the Transport Bay to evaluate Sera's loyalty and ensure compliance. Meanwhile, Ascendancy envoy Maren Caul has taken up residence at the Ascendancy Pavilion, ready to present counter-claims to the Council.

Valeria Caine — Council member and Idris's old ally — works to hold the Terrace together from the Council Chambers. And Idris Vael, retired Adept turned Prism appraiser, pretends the storm isn't coming from his shop on the Promenade.

## Tensions
- **Sovereignty vs Ascendancy**: Both Thrones want the Terrace's strategic assets. Neither will let the other claim them first.
- **Idris and Sera**: Former partners on opposite sides of a closing door. Their unresolved history makes every interaction charged.
- **Council Authority**: Valeria fights to maintain the Terrace's independence while both factions maneuver around her.
- **Loyalty Tests**: Reva is watching Sera for signs of hesitation. Sera's personal feelings for Idris are a liability.
- **The Prism Trade**: Idris monitors the Prism market for signs of escalation — if either Throne starts hoarding, war is closer than anyone admits.

## Narrative Direction
The simulation should build toward confrontation. Characters begin in their separate spheres but are drawn together by politics, duty, and personal history. Key dramatic beats to reach for:
- Sera and Idris encountering each other — the weight of what's unsaid
- Reva pressuring Sera to choose duty over sentiment
- Maren making moves in the Council that force reactions
- Valeria trying to play both sides while the ground shifts under her
- The moment someone realizes the Terrace's neutrality is already lost
